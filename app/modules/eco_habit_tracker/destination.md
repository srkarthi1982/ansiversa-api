# Eco Habit Tracker Destination

## App Name

Eco Habit Tracker

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Eco Habit Tracker should mature into a lightweight sustainable-habit workspace
that helps users discover practical eco actions, track daily completions, build
local streaks, and reflect on consistency without turning personal habit data
into account-backed surveillance by default.

The product should support everyday environmental action without becoming a
carbon-accounting platform, social challenge network, environmental compliance
tool, lifestyle scoring system, or default server-backed habit tracker.

At its destination, Eco Habit Tracker should remain simple and calm: choose
habits, complete daily actions, review consistency, and opt into deeper
features only when the privacy and governance boundaries are clear.

## Target Users

- Individuals building small sustainable routines.
- Families keeping simple household eco actions visible.
- Students practicing environmental awareness through daily action.
- Users who prefer browser-local progress over account-backed tracking.
- People who want lightweight motivation without social pressure.

## Core User Problems

- Sustainable intentions are easy to forget without a simple daily checklist.
- Users need practical actions rather than abstract environmental goals.
- Habit tracking can become invasive if completion history is stored remotely
  by default.
- Streaks should encourage consistency without becoming pressure or judgment.
- Eco apps can drift into carbon claims, compliance, social competition, or
  unsupported impact measurement.

## Final Capabilities

- Present a curated set of practical eco habits.
- Filter habits by category and search terms.
- Mark and unmark today's completed habits.
- Store completion history browser-locally by date.
- Validate stored habit IDs against the bundled habit list.
- Calculate current streak from local completion history.
- Reset filters and reset today's completions.
- Keep primary runtime workflow free of app-specific backend calls.
- Add optional reminders, goals, or sync only after explicit governance review.
- Preserve habit progress privacy by default.

## Advanced Capabilities

- Larger curated habit library.
- Optional goals for selected habits.
- Optional reminders with local-first or clearly governed account behavior.
- Export/import browser-local habit progress.
- Estimated impact ranges with transparent uncertainty.
- Account-backed streak sync with explicit opt-in.
- Private household challenges with consent controls.
- Dashboard links into broader lifestyle or wellness views.

## AI Opportunities

AI opportunities should remain limited because habit progress is personal and
eco-impact claims can be misleading.

Potential AI support includes:

- Suggesting habit categories based on user-selected goals.
- Creating non-judgmental reflection prompts.
- Helping users choose a smaller daily habit set.
- Explaining the general environmental rationale behind a habit.
- Summarizing local progress only after explicit user action.

AI must not estimate personal carbon impact as fact, shame users, infer
lifestyle behavior from progress, send habit history to external providers by
default, or make environmental compliance claims.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Mood Journal may complement routine reflection without reading habit history.
- Study Planner may schedule sustainability learning tasks if the user chooses.
- Daily Word Challenge can remain a separate daily routine product.
- File Optimizer may optimize exported habit data if exports are added.
- Eco Habit Tracker may eventually surface optional dashboard summaries through
  explicit account-backed sync.

Eco Habit Tracker owns eco habit selection, local completion, and streak
reflection. It should not absorb mood journaling, study planning, carbon
accounting, social challenges, or compliance reporting.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return to complete daily habits, review their streak, adjust filters,
reset today's progress if needed, and keep small sustainable actions visible.

The weekly value is gentle consistency: turning practical eco choices into a
repeatable routine.

## Success Criteria

- Users can browse, filter, complete, undo, and reset habit progress quickly.
- Habit completion history remains browser-local by default.
- Streaks support continuity without public ranking or pressure.
- Future sync, reminders, impact estimates, or challenges require explicit
  governance and user consent.
- Eco guidance stays practical and avoids unsupported impact certainty.
- The app remains useful without backend persistence, AI, or social features.

## Future Version Ideas

- V1.1: Expand curated habit content and categories.
- V1.2: Add export/import for local progress.
- V1.3: Add optional local reminders.
- V1.4: Add goal tracking for selected habits.
- V2: Add opt-in account sync and transparent impact estimates after
  governance review.

## Non Goals

- Do not become a carbon-accounting platform.
- Do not become an environmental compliance tool.
- Do not become a social challenge or leaderboard network by default.
- Do not become a lifestyle scoring system.
- Do not store habit progress on the server by default.
- Do not make unsupported environmental impact claims.
- Do not shame or rank users based on habit completion.
- Do not replace Mood Journal, Study Planner, or Daily Word Challenge.
- Do not send habit history to AI providers without explicit user action and
  governance review.

## Guiding Principles

- Keep sustainable action small, practical, and repeatable.
- Preserve browser-local habit progress by default.
- Treat streaks as encouragement, not judgment.
- Make sync, reminders, exports, and AI explicitly opt-in.
- Avoid unsupported carbon or compliance claims.
- Use ecosystem handoffs rather than expanding into adjacent lifestyle apps.
- Favor consistency over competition.

## Governance Notes

This document is aspirational and does not authorize immediate implementation.
Future work must be reviewed by Product Owner and Astra before development.

Any feature involving server-backed progress, reminders, impact calculations,
social challenges, AI reflection, exports, account sync, or cross-app sharing
requires explicit governance and privacy review before implementation.
