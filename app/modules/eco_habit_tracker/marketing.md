## App Summary

Eco Habit Tracker is an Ansiversa sustainability app that helps users browse practical eco habits, filter by category and search terms, mark today's completed habits, reset progress, and review a browser-local streak. The current live V1 keeps habit completion history local to the browser and uses backend catalog/overview metadata only.

The product supports small sustainable routines. It is not a carbon-accounting platform, environmental compliance tool, lifestyle scoring system, social challenge network, or server-backed habit tracker by default.

## Brand Identity

Eco Habit Tracker belongs to the Ansiversa ecosystem. Every marketing asset should feel simple, trustworthy, intelligent, professional, accessible, and globally understandable.

The brand identity should communicate:

- Simplicity: eco habits should be easy to choose and complete.
- Trust: progress stays browser-local by default.
- Intelligence: sustainability messaging should avoid unsupported precision.
- Professionalism: useful for individuals, families, students, and gentle habit builders.
- Accessibility: actions should feel practical, not guilt-heavy.
- Gentle consistency: the emotional center is small action without shame.

Avoid:

- Unsupported carbon impact or environmental compliance claims.
- Shame, guilt, ranking, or lifestyle scoring.
- Social leaderboard positioning.
- Account-backed sync or reminders as current features.
- Claims that individual tracking alone solves systemic climate problems.

## Core Value Proposition

Sustainability can feel too large or guilt-inducing. Eco Habit Tracker focuses on practical daily actions users can browse, filter, complete, undo, and reset locally.

Its strongest value is privacy through restraint. V1 does not create account-backed habit tables, calculate carbon impact, sync streaks, or send habit progress to backend systems.

## Problems We Solve

- Sustainable intentions are easy to forget.
- Users need practical actions rather than abstract climate goals.
- Habit tracking can feel invasive if progress is stored remotely by default.
- Streaks can become judgmental or competitive.
- Eco apps can overclaim impact or drift into greenwashing.
- Users need a calm routine rather than guilt.
