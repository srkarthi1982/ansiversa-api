## App Summary

Resume Builder is an Ansiversa career app that helps authenticated users create and maintain structured resume projects. Users can organize resume sections and items, preview the assembled resume, and review readiness signals without giving up authorship or career truth.

The current product is built for calm, structured resume creation. It helps students, job seekers, professionals, freelancers, and career changers turn scattered career history into organized resume content they can refine over time.

## Brand Identity

Resume Builder belongs to the Ansiversa ecosystem. Every marketing asset should feel simple, trustworthy, intelligent, professional, accessible, and globally understandable.

The brand identity should communicate:

- Simplicity: resume creation should feel structured, not overwhelming.
- Trust: the product should protect user-authored career truth.
- Intelligence: guidance should help users express real experience clearly.
- Professionalism: suitable for students, professionals, freelancers, and career changers.
- Accessibility: language and visuals should work for a global job-seeking audience.
- Calm confidence: the emotional center is readiness without false promises.

Avoid:

- Aggressive job-search panic.
- Guaranteed interview, hiring, or salary claims.
- Flashy template-first marketing that ignores substance.
- AI language that implies invented credentials or automatic success.
- Hidden-paywall messaging around meaningful resume output.

## Core Value Proposition

Resume writing is not only a formatting problem. Users need to collect real experience, organize it into meaningful sections, preview how it reads, and understand what may still be missing. Resume Builder gives users a persistent, owner-scoped workspace for resume projects, sections, items, preview, and review.

Its strongest value is trustworthy structure. The product helps users improve clarity without pretending to be a hiring engine, ATS, recruiter, or outcome predictor. It supports better resume preparation while keeping the user's real experience at the center.

## Problems We Solve

- Career history is scattered across old files, profiles, notes, and memory.
- Users do not always know which resume sections are complete.
- Resume formatting anxiety can stop users before they write useful content.
- Job seekers need preview and review before exporting or sharing.
- Students and career changers need structure when they have limited experience.
- Users need to maintain resume content over time instead of rebuilding from scratch.
- AI resume tools can invent or inflate claims if not governed carefully.
- Many resume tools create paywall frustration after users have already invested effort.

## Target Audience

### Students

Students use Resume Builder to prepare internship, first-job, academic, and scholarship resumes. They need structure, simple guidance, and confidence that early experience can be presented clearly.

### Job Seekers

Job seekers use Resume Builder to maintain resume projects and prepare for applications. They need organized sections, editable items, preview, and readiness review.

### Professionals

Professionals use Resume Builder to keep career history current before new opportunities appear. They need a durable record of achievements, roles, skills, and projects.

### Career Changers

Career changers use Resume Builder to translate existing experience into a new direction. They need clarity, structure, and future role-specific variants without losing the truth of their background.

### Freelancers And Consultants

Freelancers and consultants use Resume Builder to prepare client-facing profiles, project summaries, and professional evidence.

### Non-Native English Speakers

Non-native English speakers need clear structure and future wording support while preserving accurate meaning and avoiding invented claims.

## Visual Style Guide

### Colours

- Use the broader Ansiversa brand palette where available.
- Favor clean whites, deep navy or blue for trust, restrained green for progress, and subtle neutral grays.
- Avoid overly decorative template colors that distract from career substance.

### Lighting

- Bright, professional daylight.
- Soft home-office, campus, and workspace lighting.
- Avoid dark job-search anxiety scenes.

### Transitions

- Smooth transitions from scattered notes to structured resume sections.
- Use gentle UI zooms into preview and review.
- Avoid fast, flashy template carousel effects.

### Pacing

- Calm, purposeful, and confidence-building.
- Show progress through structure, not urgency.

### Cinematic Style

- Premium career-product commercial.
- Real people, realistic workspaces, readable interface moments.
- Show resume content abstractly or anonymized to protect privacy.
