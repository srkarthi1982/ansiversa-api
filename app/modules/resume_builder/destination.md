# Resume Builder Destination

## App Name

Resume Builder

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Resume Builder should mature into a structured resume authoring workspace that
helps users organize career evidence, build resume variants, preview polished
outputs, and review readiness before using the resume elsewhere.

The product should protect the user's authorship and career truth. It should
help users express real experience clearly without becoming an ATS, recruiter,
job-matching engine, career outcome predictor, or automated application system.

At its destination, Resume Builder should be the trusted place where a user
keeps resume projects, sections, items, previews, and review signals organized
inside the Ansiversa platform.

## Target Users

- Students preparing internships, first jobs, and academic opportunities.
- Job seekers maintaining role-specific resume versions.
- Professionals keeping career history ready for future opportunities.
- Freelancers and consultants preparing client-facing profiles.
- Career changers translating experience into clearer resume structure.

## Core User Problems

- Resume content is often scattered across old files, profiles, and notes.
- Users need structure before they can confidently polish the resume.
- Different opportunities require different resume variants.
- Users need to preview what the resume communicates before exporting or
  sharing it.
- Users need readiness signals without being misled by fake certainty or job
  guarantees.

## Final Capabilities

- Create and maintain owner-scoped resume projects.
- Organize projects into ordered sections.
- Add ordered resume items with flexible structured data.
- Enable, disable, reorder, and update resume sections.
- Preview the selected resume with enabled sections and ordered items.
- Review completion, readiness, and missing areas without treating metrics as
  hiring predictions.
- Support role-specific resume variants without duplicating unnecessary work.
- Export polished resume outputs when the preview is ready.
- Preserve user-authored content and make AI changes reviewable.
- Keep list, preview, and review data efficient and purpose-specific.

## Advanced Capabilities

- PDF export with template-aware formatting.
- Role-specific resume variants linked to job descriptions.
- AI-assisted rewrite suggestions with diffs and explanations.
- Achievement-strength review based on evidence, not exaggeration.
- Portfolio Creator handoff for selected projects and achievements.
- Version comparison across resume variants.
- Import from existing documents with explicit review before saving.
- Accessibility and ATS-readability checks.

## AI Opportunities

AI can help improve clarity, structure, and review quality, but it must never
fabricate experience or promise hiring outcomes.

Potential AI support includes:

- Suggesting stronger wording for user-provided experience.
- Explaining why a bullet point may be vague or too long.
- Recommending section organization for a target role.
- Comparing a resume project with a user-provided job description.
- Highlighting missing evidence, unclear impact, or unsupported claims.
- Producing diff-based rewrite options that preserve original meaning.

AI must not invent employers, dates, degrees, certifications, responsibilities,
metrics, skills, or achievements. AI output must remain a suggestion until the
user reviews and accepts it.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Job Description Analyzer can provide role requirements for resume tailoring.
- LinkedIn Bio Optimizer can reuse approved professional positioning.
- Portfolio Creator can receive selected resume projects or achievements.
- Grammar and Paraphrasing Assistant can improve wording without owning resume
  structure.
- AI Job Interviewer and Interview Coach can use approved resume content for
  preparation context.
- File Optimizer may optimize exported resume files.

Resume Builder owns resume structure and resume readiness. It should not absorb
job analysis, interview practice, portfolio publishing, or profile optimization.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return when they apply for roles, update experience, add projects, revise
career positioning, tailor a resume to a new opportunity, or review whether a
resume version is complete enough to use.

The repeat value comes from maintaining career evidence over time, not from
constantly rewriting the same resume.

## Success Criteria

- Users can build and maintain structured resume projects without leaving the
  platform.
- Resume sections and items remain easy to create, update, delete, preview,
  and review.
- Preview output reflects the selected resume structure accurately.
- Readiness review helps users improve completeness without pretending to be a
  hiring decision.
- AI suggestions preserve authorship, truth, and reviewability.
- Resume Builder hands off to adjacent career products without absorbing their
  responsibilities.
- The product remains useful even without AI or external integrations.

## Non Goals

- Do not become an ATS.
- Do not become a recruiting platform.
- Do not become a job board.
- Do not automate job applications.
- Do not guarantee interviews, offers, rankings, or hiring outcomes.
- Do not fabricate or inflate user qualifications.
- Do not provide legal, immigration, or employment-law advice.
- Do not replace LinkedIn Bio Optimizer, Portfolio Creator, Career Planner, or
  Job Description Analyzer.
- Do not treat AI rewrite output as user-approved content by default.

## Guiding Principles

- Preserve user authorship and career truth.
- Improve clarity without inventing evidence.
- Keep resume structure separate from job matching and recruiting decisions.
- Treat readiness metrics as review signals, not objective judgments.
- Make AI suggestions diffable, explainable, and optional.
- Let adjacent career apps contribute context through explicit handoffs.
- Keep the resume useful as a standalone user-owned artifact.
