## Purpose and problem

People often need a simple manual view of what they own and owe without surrendering banking credentials. Target users are individuals and households maintaining periodic personal records.

## Landscape and common patterns

Personal-finance tools commonly combine account aggregation, budgeting, investment dashboards, and net-worth history. Manual trackers are valued for privacy and control but often suffer from tedious updates, unclear currency handling, and snapshots that change retrospectively. Paid products frequently gate history, reports, or account connections. AI trends focus on categorization and advice, neither of which is required here.

## Ansiversa opportunity and UX direction

Use explicit asset/liability labels, manual balances, immutable snapshots, compact mobile cards, clear inclusion/archive controls, and currency-separated totals. Avoid live-price claims, cross-currency aggregation, performance labels, bank credential storage, advice, scoring, and trading workflows.

## Product questions

Destination approval, future exports, and any custom-category capability remain open decisions.
