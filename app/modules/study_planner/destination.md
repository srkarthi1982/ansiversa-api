# Study Planner Destination

## App Name

Study Planner

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Study Planner should mature into a focused study planning workspace that helps
users turn learning goals into plans, tasks, study logs, and reviewable
progress.

The product should help users plan and reflect on study work without becoming a
calendar replacement, LMS, tutoring system, proctoring tool, exam predictor, or
automated education authority.

At its destination, Study Planner should make the path from intention to
consistent study visible: what the user plans to learn, what tasks remain, how
much focused work has happened, and what should be reviewed next.

## Target Users

- Students planning coursework, revision, and exam preparation.
- Self-learners turning goals into recurring study tasks.
- Professionals preparing certifications or skills upgrades.
- Users who need a lightweight study workflow separate from full course
  tracking.
- Learners who want study effort and reflection preserved over time.

## Core User Problems

- Study goals are easy to write down and hard to turn into action.
- Learners often lose track of tasks, due dates, and actual effort.
- Progress feels unclear when study logs and tasks are disconnected.
- Users need review signals without being judged by the app.
- Study planning should support discipline without becoming surveillance or
  academic control.

## Final Capabilities

- Create owner-scoped study plans with subject, goal, dates, and status.
- Add tasks under plans with priority, due date, and completion status.
- Update plans and tasks as the study path changes.
- Record dated study logs with minutes, focus, and reflection.
- Optionally connect logs to specific tasks.
- Review active plans, completed tasks, total minutes, completion rate, and
  recent logs.
- Preserve logs as historical records of study effort.
- Support reminders or calendar export only through explicit user action.
- Connect to learning apps for user-approved study inputs.
- Keep the workflow lightweight enough for repeated weekly use.

## Advanced Capabilities

- Calendar export for selected study tasks.
- Reminder-ready task metadata and notification controls.
- Workload balancing across dates and subjects.
- Study streaks and habit reflection without punitive scoring.
- Course Tracker handoff from unfinished modules.
- Quiz or Memory Trainer feedback loops for review planning.
- AI-assisted task breakdown from user-provided goals.
- Subject-level analytics across plans, tasks, and logs.

## AI Opportunities

AI may help users break down goals, rebalance workload, and reflect on study
patterns, but it must remain optional and user-controlled.

Potential AI support includes:

- Suggesting tasks from a user-provided study goal.
- Recommending a lighter or more balanced schedule.
- Summarizing study logs into progress reflections.
- Identifying neglected subjects or overdue tasks.
- Suggesting review sessions based on completed work.
- Explaining plan risks without predicting exam outcomes.

AI must not guarantee grades, predict exam success, replace tutors, make
academic decisions, or silently reschedule a user's study plan.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Course Tracker can send unfinished modules into Study Planner as tasks.
- Quiz can provide review needs after user-approved results.
- Memory Trainer can provide spaced-review signals.
- Smart Textbook Scanner can send reviewed textbook notes for study planning.
- AI Notes Summarizer can hand off summaries to be reviewed.
- Daily Word Challenge may contribute vocabulary practice plans.

Study Planner owns planning, tasks, logs, and review. It should not absorb
course structure, quiz delivery, flashcard training, textbook extraction, or
note summarization.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return weekly to plan study work, update task status, log focused
minutes, review progress, and adjust upcoming effort.

The product's value is recurring accountability: helping users see whether
their study intention is becoming actual work.

## Success Criteria

- Users can turn goals into plans, tasks, logs, and review metrics.
- Study tasks are easy to create, update, complete, and delete.
- Logs remain historical records and support honest reflection.
- Review metrics motivate planning without becoming judgmental or predictive.
- Ecosystem handoffs help users plan study work without merging products.
- AI assistance, if added, remains explainable, optional, and reversible.
- The app remains useful without calendar, reminder, or AI dependencies.

## Non Goals

- Do not become an LMS.
- Do not become a calendar replacement.
- Do not become a tutoring platform.
- Do not become a proctoring or surveillance tool.
- Do not predict grades or exam outcomes.
- Do not guarantee academic performance.
- Do not replace Course Tracker, Quiz, Memory Trainer, Smart Textbook Scanner,
  or AI Notes Summarizer.
- Do not automatically reschedule user work without explicit approval.
- Do not treat study metrics as moral judgment or academic authority.

## Guiding Principles

- Support user agency rather than academic control.
- Turn goals into manageable tasks and honest reflection.
- Treat logs as historical study records.
- Keep review metrics useful, calm, and non-judgmental.
- Make reminders and calendar connections explicit.
- Let adjacent learning apps keep their own responsibilities.
- Use AI only to suggest, explain, and assist with user review.
