## Purpose

Emergency Checklist studies preparedness checklist patterns for households and individuals who want reusable plans before a fire, storm, outage, medical event, vehicle issue, travel disruption, or similar emergency.

## Problem Statement

Preparedness guidance is widely available, but users often need a private way to convert guidance into their own recurring checklists, track completion, and reset checklists for repeated reviews.

## Target Users

- Individuals maintaining home, travel, vehicle, or personal readiness lists.
- Families coordinating child, elder, pet, medical, or home emergency preparation.
- Workers preparing workplace or commute checklists.

## Competitor Landscape

- Ready.gov and FEMA publish plan and kit guidance for households and communities.
- American Red Cross provides emergency kit and preparedness education.
- State emergency management sites provide regional checklist examples.
- Notes and task apps can store lists but usually lack emergency-specific grouping, reset behavior, or completion summaries.

## Common Market Features

- Preparedness plans.
- Supply kit lists.
- Household contacts and meeting-place prompts.
- Vehicle and pet supplies.
- Printable or downloadable checklists.
- Periodic review reminders in some tools.

## User Love Signals

- Clear checklists reduce anxiety before emergencies.
- Reusable lists help users repeat preparedness reviews.
- Simple completion tracking is easier than reading long guides every time.

## Complaints And Friction

- Public guidance can be broad and hard to personalize.
- Printed checklists become stale.
- Generic task apps do not separate preparedness context from normal errands.
- Emergency apps can overpromise with alerts or response features users cannot rely on.

## Pricing And Paywall Observations

Public preparedness guidance is commonly free. Commercial emergency kits and training products are paid, but a checklist planner should not require marketplace, affiliate, or lead-generation behavior.

## AI Trends

AI may eventually help draft checklist templates, but this V1 intentionally avoids AI advice because emergency preparedness can be high-stakes and must remain user-authored and reviewable.

## UX Patterns

- Category-based hazard grouping.
- Checklist progress percentages.
- Resettable recurring lists.
- Clear completed and remaining counts.
- Archive for stale checklists without deleting history.

## Ansiversa Opportunities

- Provide a private preparedness workspace inside the daily-life suite.
- Keep checklists reusable, resettable, and easy to review.
- Make completion state transparent without turning the app into emergency-response software.

## Avoid List

- SOS buttons.
- Live alerts.
- GPS rescue flows.
- Dispatch or first responder claims.
- Disaster monitoring.
- Medical advice.
- External APIs.
- AI emergency recommendations.

## Product Questions

- Should reminders be handled by a platform-wide notification system later?
