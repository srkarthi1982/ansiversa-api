## Purpose

Understand lightweight reminder tools for birthdays, anniversaries, and meaningful annual occasions.

## Problem Statement

Important dates are easy to miss when they live in memory, phone contacts, calendars, or chat threads. Users need a focused place to save the date, relationship, contact details, notes, and gift ideas.

## Target Users

- Families tracking birthdays and anniversaries.
- Friends remembering meaningful dates.
- Professionals tracking work anniversaries and graduation milestones.

## Competitor Landscape

- Phone contact birthday fields and native calendar reminders.
- Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Outlook Calendar.
- Reminder apps such as Todoist, TickTick, and Microsoft To Do.
- Dedicated birthday reminder apps in mobile stores.

## Common Market Features

- Annual recurrence.
- Upcoming reminders.
- Contact fields.
- Favourite or important labels.
- Notes and gift ideas.

## User Love Signals

- Simple list of upcoming dates.
- Clear days remaining.
- Reusable annual recurrence.
- Quick editing and archiving.

## Complaints And Friction

- Too many ads.
- Unwanted social integrations.
- Confusing notification permissions.
- Calendar clutter.

## Pricing And Paywall Observations

Most general reminder apps use freemium sync, notification, and collaboration paywalls. Dedicated birthday apps commonly monetize ads, widgets, and premium reminders.

## AI Trends

AI gift ideas and message drafts are common but outside V1. Any future AI should be optional, editable, and clearly non-automatic.

## UX Patterns

- Upcoming-first lists.
- Date countdowns.
- Month distribution.
- Favourite contacts.
- Archive completed or inactive records.

## Ansiversa Opportunities

- Keep V1 private and practical.
- Separate acknowledgement from deletion so annual recurrence remains useful.
- Avoid automatic message-sending claims.

## Avoid List

- Do not send messages automatically.
- Do not scrape contacts.
- Do not imply official calendar sync or notification delivery in V1.
