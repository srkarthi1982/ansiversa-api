# Visiting Card Maker Destination

## App Name

Visiting Card Maker

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Visiting Card Maker should mature into a focused professional card design
workspace for creating, reviewing, reusing, and exporting polished digital
business cards.

The product should help users turn essential identity and contact details into
clear professional cards without becoming a print shop, CRM, brand management
suite, or public contact network.

At its destination, the app should feel fast, practical, and trustworthy:
users can create a card, preview it, save variations, duplicate an existing
card for another role or company, and export or share it with confidence.

## Target Users

- Professionals who need a polished contact card for networking.
- Freelancers who need role-specific or client-facing card variations.
- Small business owners who need simple professional cards without design
  software.
- Students and early-career users preparing professional identity materials.
- Consultants and independent workers who need reusable card records.
- Founders, creators, service professionals, and event networkers who need both printed-card readiness and digital sharing clarity.
- Users who want QR or vCard sharing without accidentally creating public contact analytics.

## Core User Problems

- Professional contact details are often scattered across profiles, documents,
  and email signatures.
- Users need attractive cards without learning complex design tools.
- A single person may need several card variations for different roles,
  companies, or audiences.
- Users need to review contact accuracy before exporting or sharing a card.
- Lightweight card creation should not require print-vendor accounts or public
  hosted profiles.
- Attractive cards can fail if text is too small, contrast is weak, or QR codes are not scannable.
- Static and dynamic QR destinations need clear expectations before users print or share a card.

## Final Capabilities

- Create and save owner-scoped professional card records.
- Keep identity, role, company, contact, address, and tagline details together
  in one editable workflow.
- Choose from a curated set of professional templates.
- Preview the card while editing so content and design stay connected.
- Edit saved cards without losing the original professional context.
- Duplicate cards to create variations for different roles or companies.
- Delete cards the user no longer needs.
- Export cards from the browser in practical formats for sharing or printing.
- Support consistent card rendering across desktop, tablet, and mobile.
- Preserve clear feedback for create, update, duplicate, delete, and export
  actions.

## Advanced Capabilities

- vCard export for contact import workflows.
- PDF export with print-ready dimensions.
- QR code support for contact sharing.
- Public share links with explicit user opt-in.
- Brand presets for colors, typography, and logo placement.
- Print-readiness checks for text size, contrast, margins, bleed, and QR scanability.
- Explicit QR destination review for vCard, portfolio, user URL, or future Ansiversa profile handoffs.
- Template versioning so older saved cards remain stable.
- Lightweight share analytics for explicitly shared links.
- Portfolio Creator handoff for professional identity consistency.

## AI Opportunities

AI may eventually assist with card clarity, but it must remain optional and
reviewable.

Potential AI support includes:

- Suggesting concise professional taglines from user-provided context.
- Checking whether contact details appear complete and readable.
- Recommending layout-friendly wording when text is too long.
- Suggesting alternate professional titles without inventing credentials.
- Explaining why a card may be visually crowded or unclear.

AI must not invent names, companies, roles, certifications, contact details, or
business claims. Any AI-assisted text must remain visible for user review before
it becomes part of a saved card.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Portfolio Creator may reuse professional identity details when the user
  explicitly chooses to share them.
- Resume Builder may provide career identity context, but Visiting Card Maker
  should not absorb resume authoring.
- LinkedIn Bio Optimizer may align professional positioning, but it should not
  publish or manage cards.
- QR Code Creator may support explicit QR generation for contact sharing.
- File Optimizer may help optimize exported card images or PDFs after export.

These connections should be handoffs, not ownership transfers. Visiting Card
Maker owns card creation and card review only.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return when they change roles, update contact details, prepare for
networking, create a client-specific variation, refresh a card design, or
export a card for an event.

The weekly value is not constant daily use. It is reliable professional reuse
whenever identity or networking context changes.

## Success Criteria

- Users can create a polished card in a short focused workflow.
- Saved cards are easy to edit, duplicate, delete, preview, and export.
- Card data remains owner-scoped and private by default.
- Exports represent the card accurately across supported devices.
- Card previews remain legible, print-aware, and scan-aware before export.
- Template choices improve presentation without overwhelming the user.
- The product never requires print orders, hosted profiles, or public sharing
  to be useful.
- Future features strengthen card clarity rather than expanding into CRM or
  brand-suite territory.

## Journey Progress

Current Position: 64 / 100
Destination: 100 / 100
Remaining Journey: 36 / 100

This estimate describes product maturity, not feature completion.

Visiting Card Maker already has a live workflow for creating, editing,
duplicating, deleting, previewing, and browser-exporting cards. The remaining

professional identity handoffs, and stronger review guidance.

- V1.2: Add PDF export with print-friendly sizing.
- V1.3: Add QR code handoff and optional contact QR placement.
- V1.4: Add brand presets for repeated card styles.
- V2: Add opt-in share links with privacy controls and lightweight analytics.

## Non Goals

- Do not become a CRM.
- Do not become a lead-generation platform.
- Do not become a business identity management suite.
- Do not become a print fulfillment service.
- Do not become a public contact directory.
- Do not become a team brand-governance platform.
- Do not scrape or publish user contact data.
- Do not silently create shareable public profiles.
- Do not invent professional claims or credentials with AI.
- Do not imply print fulfillment, color accuracy, paper quality, or shipping outcomes unless Ansiversa owns that workflow.
- Do not collect contact scans, lead analytics, or CRM data without explicit consent and governance.

## Guiding Principles

- Keep the product focused on professional card creation.
- Treat contact details as private user-owned information.
- Require explicit user action before exporting or sharing card data.
- Preserve editability for saved cards and variations.
- Prioritize readability, scanability, and contact accuracy over decorative design.
- Prefer clear presentation over excessive design controls.
- Keep ecosystem connections explicit and user-driven.
- Make AI helpful only when it improves clarity without inventing identity.
