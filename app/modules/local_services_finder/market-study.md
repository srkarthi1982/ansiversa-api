## Purpose

Local Services Finder studies how people keep track of trusted local providers such as electricians, plumbers, doctors, pharmacies, car repair shops, tutors, cleaners, movers, lawyers, accountants, salons, and restaurants.

## Problem Statement

Public marketplaces and map products help discover providers, but they are not designed as a private memory of trusted contacts already known to the user. Users often need a lightweight directory for phone numbers, addresses, notes, ratings, and preferred providers without live search, booking, reviews, ads, maps, GPS, or route planning.

## Target Users

- Individuals maintaining a personal trusted-provider list.
- Families tracking household, health, vehicle, education, and care contacts.
- Small-business users tracking nearby suppliers, professional services, and emergency contacts.
- New residents building a private local services directory.

## Competitor Landscape

- Google Business Profile and Google Maps focus on public business discovery, map visibility, business profiles, reviews, and search/map presence. Source: https://business.google.com/us/business-profile/
- Yelp focuses on public local business pages, reviews, photos, and paid business advertising. Source: https://business.yelp.com/
- Angi focuses on home services, local pros, job requests, and marketplace matching. Source: https://www.angi.com/
- Thumbtack focuses on finding and hiring local professionals for specific jobs. Source: https://www.thumbtack.com/
- Yellow Pages and public directories focus on broad business listings and phone-directory discovery. Source: https://www.yellowpages.com/

## Common Market Features

- Business name, category, contact details, address, and website.
- Public search by area, service type, rating, or review.
- Reviews, photos, map pins, and business hours.
- Lead generation, booking, quotes, or marketplace workflows.
- Paid listings, ads, or promoted placement.

## User Love Signals

- Users value reliable contact details and personal notes.
- Ratings and preferred/favorite indicators help recall trusted providers.
- Category filters make service lists easier to scan.
- Archived or inactive contacts should be preserved without cluttering active lists.

## Complaints And Friction

- Public results can be noisy, ad-heavy, or unfamiliar.
- Reviews may not reflect a user's own trusted experience.
- Booking and quote workflows can be too heavy when users only need a phone number.
- Map-first products may be unnecessary for already-known providers.
- Users may not want to expose personal provider notes to third-party platforms.

## Pricing And Paywall Observations

Discovery and listing platforms often monetize ads, promoted listings, lead generation, business tools, or contractor subscriptions. Local Services Finder should avoid marketplace or lead-generation behavior and remain a private user-owned directory.

## AI Trends

AI is appearing in local search summaries, lead matching, and service recommendations. For this product, AI should not be claimed in V1. Future AI could summarize private notes or suggest categories only after approval, without replacing user judgment or implying public search.

## UX Patterns Worth Studying

- Category-based directory views.
- Favorites/preferred provider markers.
- Rating filters.
- Contact and address detail screens.
- Archive/restore for stale providers.

## Ansiversa Opportunities

- Build a private directory, not a public search engine.
- Preserve trusted provider notes inside the Ansiversa account.
- Keep the workflow lightweight and owner-scoped.
- Use shared drawers, confirmations, pagination, generated contracts, and persistent shell routing.

## Avoid List

- Do not add Google Maps, GPS, directions, route planning, booking, quotes, marketplace matching, third-party directory search, public reviews, or external APIs.
- Do not copy competitor listing layouts, review models, ranking systems, or ad language.

## Product Questions

- Should V2 support importing contacts from a user-approved file?
- Should provider categories include optional defaults?
- Should reminders for follow-up contacts use a future shared notification service?
- Should emergency contacts remain separate from this app?
