## App Summary

Contract Generator is an Ansiversa business app that helps authenticated users organize contract projects, draft documents, clauses, and activity history. The current live V1 stores owner-scoped draft records with lightweight dashboard summaries and detail endpoints for editable contract work.

The product is a structured draft workspace. It is not a law firm, legal advice product, e-signature platform, contract lifecycle management system, automated legal-review engine, or jurisdiction-specific legal service.

## Brand Identity

Contract Generator belongs to the Ansiversa ecosystem. Every marketing asset should feel simple, trustworthy, intelligent, professional, accessible, and globally understandable.

The brand identity should communicate:

- Simplicity: contract draft work should be organized.
- Trust: legal boundaries should be visible.
- Intelligence: clauses and history should support review, not replace counsel.
- Professionalism: useful for freelancers, consultants, small businesses, founders, and operators.
- Accessibility: non-legal users should understand draft status.
- Responsible structure: the emotional center is preparation for review.

Avoid:

- Legal advice, enforceability, compliance, or jurisdiction-specific claims.
- AI legal drafting as current functionality.
- E-signature or export claims as current features.
- Auto-finalizing, auto-sending, or auto-signing.
- Using competitor clauses, templates, or legal wording.

## Core Value Proposition

Contract work often begins with scattered notes, old files, copied text, and unclear status. Contract Generator gives users a structured place for contract projects, documents, clauses, and history so draft work stays organized and reviewable.

Its strongest value is responsible boundary-setting. The app supports drafting records, not legal judgment, enforceability decisions, signatures, or automated legal review.

## Problems We Solve

- Contract work is scattered across files and notes.
- Projects, documents, clauses, and review activity become disconnected.
- Reusable clauses need review context.
- Users need draft organization before professional review.
- Legal tools can create false confidence if boundaries are unclear.
- Contract text may be sensitive and should not appear casually in lists.
