# Emergency Contacts Organizer Marketing

## Product Promise

Emergency Contacts Organizer helps users keep important contact information together, searchable, and quick to access.

## Target Audiences

- Parents and caregivers
- Households and residents
- Travellers
- Users managing medical, school, insurance, workplace, or roadside contacts

## Benefits

- Keeps essential contacts in one protected workspace.
- Surfaces favourite and primary contacts.
- Makes phone and email actions easy to find.
- Helps users spot missing information.
- Keeps responsible boundaries around emergency-response claims.

## Feature Highlights

- Contact CRUD with relationship, category, phone, email, location, address, notes, priority, favourite, and primary fields.
- Default and custom categories.
- Search and category/favourite filters.
- Contact details drawer.
- Lightweight insights.
- Mobile-friendly layout.

## Responsible Wording

Use: organize, store, find, review, quick reference, important contacts.  
Avoid: rescue, dispatch, guaranteed help, emergency response, life-saving, official verification, automatic calling.

## Short-Form Concepts

- "Important contacts, kept together."
- "Family, medical, school, insurance, and assistance contacts in one place."
- "Find the right number faster, then contact services yourself."

## Video Direction

Show a calm household scene: a user adds family, doctor, school, and insurance contacts, marks one as favourite, searches the list, and taps a phone number. End with clear safety wording that Ansiversa organizes information and does not replace official emergency services.

## Storyboard

1. A user checks scattered notes and messages.
2. Emergency Contacts Organizer opens to the contact list.
3. The user creates a medical contact and adds phone/email details.
4. The user marks a family contact as favourite.
5. The user searches and opens contact details.
6. Closing frame: "Important contacts, kept together."

## CTA Ideas

- Explore
- Open contacts
- Organize important contacts

## SEO Notes

Potential themes: emergency contact organizer, family emergency contacts, important contacts app, private contact organizer, household contact list.

## Guardrails

Marketing must never imply the app contacts emergency services, verifies emergency numbers, replaces local authorities, provides medical advice, or guarantees help.
