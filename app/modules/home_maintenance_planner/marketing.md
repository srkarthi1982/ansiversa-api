# Home Maintenance Planner Marketing

## Product Promise

Home Maintenance Planner helps people keep household maintenance tasks, due dates, service notes, and costs organized in one private workspace.

## Target Audiences

- Families coordinating recurring home tasks.
- Homeowners and villa residents maintaining larger spaces.
- Renters tracking appliance care and minor upkeep.
- People who want provider and reference details close to each task.

## Key Benefits

- See overdue and upcoming work clearly.
- Track one-time and recurring maintenance.
- Keep provider, reference, cost, and notes together.
- Review maintenance patterns by area, category, and priority.
- Stay focused on planning without contractor marketplace complexity.

## Feature Highlights

- Task CRUD with search, filters, and sorting.
- Default and user-managed areas and categories.
- Weekly, monthly, quarterly, six-monthly, yearly, and custom recurrence.
- Complete/reopen flow with recurring next due dates.
- Estimated versus actual cost tracking.
- Dashboard and insights.

## Responsible Claims

Use: planning tool, personal records, due-date visibility, maintenance organization.

Avoid: guaranteed safety, professional inspection, regulatory compliance, emergency repair, contractor certification, automatic fault diagnosis, damage prevention guarantees.

## Promotional Concepts

- "Keep home maintenance from disappearing into old notes."
- "A practical planner for the work every home needs."
- "Track due dates, service details, and costs without heavy property software."

## Video Direction

Storyboard: A user notices scattered reminders in messages and notes. They open Home Maintenance Planner, create an AC servicing task, add provider details, mark a completed water-tank cleaning task, and review overdue/upcoming work. End with the app still positioned as a planner, not a professional service.

## CTA Ideas

- Explore
- Plan maintenance
- Review tasks

Primary platform CTA remains `Explore`.

## Guardrails

Do not use fear-based messaging. Do not imply that missing a reminder will necessarily cause damage or danger. Do not show hazardous repair guidance. Encourage qualified professionals for specialized or risky work.
