## Purpose

This document captures external market intelligence for Trip Cost Calculator. It is research only and does not define implementation commitments.

## Problem Statement

Travel costs are often spread across fuel, tolls, parking, food, accommodation, tickets, and transport receipts. Users may estimate costs before a trip and then lose track of actual spending afterward. Existing travel planners often focus on itinerary and booking, while expense tools focus on general budgets rather than trip-level comparison.

## Target Users

- Road-trip planners.
- Families and groups splitting travel costs informally.
- Commuters comparing recurring routes.
- Frequent travelers reviewing trip budgets.
- Users preparing notes before budgeting or reimbursement discussions.

## Competitor Landscape

- TravelMath and similar calculators estimate distance, fuel, and travel cost.
- GasBuddy trip cost tools focus on fuel price estimates and route context.
- Wanderlog and TripIt focus on itinerary organization with some budgeting patterns.
- Splitwise and travel expense splitters focus on shared balances.
- General expense trackers capture spending but may not model trip distance, travelers, and category comparison together.

## Common Market Features

- Fuel cost estimation.
- Distance and route assumptions.
- Category budgets.
- Per-person totals.
- Shared trip expenses.
- Currency entry.
- Export or report preparation.
- Receipt attachment in richer tools.

## User Love Signals

- Quick answers for “what will this trip cost?”
- Per-person clarity for family or group trips.
- Simple category totals.
- Reusable trip records.
- Side-by-side comparison across trips.

## Complaints And Friction

- Route and fuel estimates can be inaccurate.
- Currency conversion can be confusing.
- Shared expenses can become too complex.
- Booking apps may hide true total cost.
- Generic expense tools lack trip context.

## Pricing And Paywall Observations

Simple calculators are usually free. Travel planning platforms may monetize itinerary collaboration, booking referrals, or premium organization. Expense and split tools may charge for advanced exports, OCR, sync, or group features.

## AI Trends

AI may summarize trip costs, detect missing categories, or turn receipts into structured entries. These features require privacy review because travel records can reveal sensitive movement patterns.

## UX Patterns Worth Studying

- Fast trip setup.
- Clear category chips.
- Cost per traveler.
- Cost per kilometer or mile.
- Simple comparison tables.
- Exportable summaries.

## Ansiversa Opportunities

- Build a private trip-cost ledger rather than a booking marketplace.
- Connect naturally with Parking Expense Tracker, Car Pool, Rent a Car, and Vehicle Maintenance Tracker in future governed workflows.
- Keep V1 deterministic and user-entered.

## Avoid List

- Do not copy competitor calculators, route pricing, or proprietary formulas.
- Do not claim live fuel prices or official route estimates without approved integrations.
- Do not turn V1 into accounting, tax, reimbursement approval, or payment splitting.

## Product Questions

- Should future versions support miles and kilometers explicitly?
- Should currency normalization be added after V1?
- Should receipt attachments or exports be prioritized first?
