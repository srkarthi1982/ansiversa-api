## App Summary

AI Job Interviewer is an Ansiversa career app that helps authenticated users organize job-specific interview practice through sessions, questions, written answers, confidence tracking, and progress results. The current live V1 stores owner-scoped preparation records and dashboard counters so users can keep practice structured and reviewable.

The product is built for ethical preparation, not live interview assistance, recruiter scoring, surveillance, or job-outcome prediction.

## Brand Identity

AI Job Interviewer belongs to the Ansiversa ecosystem. Every marketing asset should feel simple, trustworthy, intelligent, professional, accessible, and globally understandable.

The brand identity should communicate:

- Simplicity: interview practice should feel organized, not scattered.
- Trust: private career preparation should remain user-owned.
- Intelligence: feedback should guide practice without judging employability.
- Professionalism: suitable for students, job seekers, professionals, and coaches.
- Accessibility: users should feel supported across experience levels.
- Ethical confidence: the emotional center is preparation over prediction.

Avoid:

- Real-time cheating or live copilot positioning.
- Guaranteed interviews, offers, hiring scores, or recruiter approval.
- Surveillance, psychometric, or employer-screening language.
- Voice/video claims as current features.
- Overconfident AI judgment.

## Core Value Proposition

Interview preparation often lives in scattered notes, generic question lists, and vague confidence. AI Job Interviewer turns preparation into a structured workflow: create a session, add role-specific context, organize questions, write answers, track confidence, and review progress feedback.

Its strongest value is governed practice. The product supports preparation and learning without becoming a hiring authority, real-time interview assistant, recruiter score, or outcome predictor.

## Problems We Solve

- Interview notes and questions are scattered across files and memory.
- Users need practice context tied to role, company, experience level, and interview type.
- Answers need confidence and status tracking.
- Progress feedback should remain reviewable instead of arbitrary.
- Job seekers need private practice before human interviews.
- AI interview tools can drift into unethical live assistance.
- Users need preparation guidance without job-offer promises.
