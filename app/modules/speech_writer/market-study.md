## Problem Statement

People need speeches for weddings, ceremonies, school events, business
presentations, pitches, tributes, awards, and public speaking moments. The
problem is not only drafting text. Users need the right tone, structure,
audience fit, length, pacing, memorable stories, authenticity, and delivery
confidence.

The market overlaps with AI writing tools, public speaking coaching, text-to-
speech, voiceover tools, and presentation software. Users want help saying
something meaningful without sounding generic or artificial.

## Target Users

- Students preparing class speeches or debates.
- Professionals giving presentations, pitches, or internal remarks.
- Wedding speakers writing vows, toasts, or ceremony speeches.
- Leaders preparing keynotes, announcements, or staff messages.
- Community speakers preparing tributes, awards, or introductions.
- Non-native speakers improving clarity and tone.
- Users who need a script plus practice support.
- Speech-adjacent users of Presentation Designer and Interview Coach.

## Competitor Landscape

### Direct Competitors

- ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini: General AI assistants commonly used to draft,
  rewrite, shorten, and adapt speeches.
- Grammarly AI Writer: Broad writing assistant that can generate and polish
  content across use cases, with tone and voice-related features.
- Jasper: Marketing and business AI writing platform with brand voice,
  campaigns, and content workflows that can include speech-like scripts.
- Typli AI Speech Writer and small dedicated generators: Lightweight tools that
  generate speeches from topic, tone, and occasion.
- Speech Generator mobile apps: App-store tools focused on creating scripts for
  events such as weddings, business talks, and ceremonies.
- Toastmasters: Not a speech-writing app, but a major public speaking training
  ecosystem focused on confidence, delivery, feedback, and practice.
- Speechify, ElevenLabs, Murf, and AI voice tools: Adjacent tools that turn
  speech scripts into spoken audio or practice voiceovers.

### Indirect Competitors

- Human speechwriters, coaches, and editors.
- Public speaking courses and clubs.
- Presentation Designer and slide tools.
- Google Docs, Microsoft Word, and template sites.
- YouTube speaking advice and sample speeches.
- Wedding planning sites and ceremony resources.
- Teleprompter apps and rehearsal tools.

### AI-Based Alternatives

- ChatGPT: Flexible for outlines, drafts, tone variants, jokes, stories, and
  length adjustments, but requires user direction and fact checking.
- Claude: Strong for nuanced tone, long source notes, and preserving personal
  voice.
- Gemini: Useful for Google Docs-adjacent drafting and presentation workflows.
- Voice AI tools: Can read speeches aloud, create voiceovers, and support
  rehearsal, but may introduce authenticity and consent questions.

AI assistants compete at draft speed. Dedicated speech tools win when they guide
occasion, audience, message, timing, and delivery readiness.

## Common Market Features

- Speech generation by occasion.
- Tone and audience controls.
- Length or speaking-time controls.
- Outline and full draft modes.
- Opening, body, transition, and closing sections.
- Joke, story, quote, or call-to-action suggestions.
- Rewrite, shorten, expand, and simplify.
- Speaker notes or teleprompter-style output.
- Text-to-speech preview.
- Practice timing and pacing support.
- Export to document, PDF, or presentation workflow.

## What Users Appear to Love

- Escaping the blank page before an important event.
- Tone help for emotional or formal speeches.
- Length control for strict time slots.
- Multiple versions to compare.
- Clear structure that feels easy to deliver.
- Rehearsal support and read-aloud previews.
- Public speaking communities that provide human feedback.
- Tools that help preserve personal voice.

## Common Complaints / Friction

- AI speeches can sound generic, sentimental, or over-polished.
- Generated jokes or emotional lines can feel inappropriate.
- Personal stories may be invented or distorted.
- Speech length may not match actual speaking pace.
- Users need delivery coaching, not just a script.
- Public speakers may worry about sounding inauthentic.
- Voice cloning and AI voiceovers raise consent and identity concerns.
- Writing tools may not understand the occasion's cultural norms.

## Pricing and Paywall Observations

- General AI assistants include speech writing inside broader paid AI plans.
- Grammarly and Jasper monetize writing assistance, brand voice, and team
  workflows rather than speech writing alone.
- Dedicated speech generators often use free trials, ads, subscriptions, or
  limited generations.
- Speechify and AI voice tools monetize premium voices, languages, speed, voice
  cloning, dubbing, and business features.
- Toastmasters uses a membership/community model rather than software pricing.

The market opportunity is not raw generation. It is guided, authentic speech
drafting with practical rehearsal support and clear boundaries around voice and
identity.

## AI Capability Trends

- AI can quickly generate speech outlines and drafts by occasion.
- Voice and TTS tools are making script preview easier.
- Personalized voice and brand voice features are growing, but consent concerns
  are rising.
- Users increasingly expect tone, length, and audience controls.
- Presentation tools and speech tools are converging around script, slides, and
  delivery practice.
- Ethical pressure is increasing around AI mimicking named writers, speakers, or
  voices without consent.

AI should help users shape their own message rather than imitate someone else's
style or fabricate personal moments.

## UX Patterns Worth Studying

- Start from occasion, audience, goal, tone, and time limit.
- Collect personal facts and stories before drafting.
- Generate outline before full speech.
- Show estimated speaking time.
- Section-level editing.
- Read-aloud or rehearsal preview.
- Delivery checklist: pace, pauses, opening, closing, and transitions.
- Export to Presentation Designer or document.
- Clear warnings against fabricated personal stories or unauthorized imitation.

## Opportunities for Ansiversa

- Position Speech Writer as authentic message drafting and rehearsal support.
- Connect naturally with Presentation Designer, Interview Coach, Meeting Minutes
  AI, Proposal Writer, AI Translator & Tone Fixer, and Prompt Builder through
  approved platform boundaries.
- Help users preserve their own voice with user-provided stories and facts.
- Keep occasion and audience context central.
- Support timed drafts and delivery review.
- Avoid named-person imitation and voice cloning by default.

## What Ansiversa Should Avoid

- Do not copy competitor speech templates, scripts, rhetorical structures, UI,
  or generated examples.
- Do not imitate living people, famous speakers, or protected writer styles
  without consent.
- Do not let AI invent personal memories, credentials, or event details.
- Do not promise emotional impact or audience reaction.
- Do not over-polish speeches until they sound inauthentic.
- Do not add voice cloning or audio generation without separate approval.
- Do not add global abstractions or shared components from this research alone.
