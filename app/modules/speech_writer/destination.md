# Speech Writer Destination

## App Name

Speech Writer

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Speech Writer should mature into a focused prepared-speaking workspace that
helps users plan speech occasions, draft speeches, reuse proven structures,
review readiness, and track revision or delivery history.

The product should support thoughtful speech preparation without becoming a
generic document editor, teleprompter, event management platform, public
speaking marketplace, or unmanaged AI speech factory.

At its destination, Speech Writer should keep the occasion, audience, message,
draft, reusable templates, and revision context connected so users can prepare
speeches with clarity and confidence.

## Target Users

- Professionals preparing workplace remarks, announcements, and presentations.
- Students writing speeches for classes, events, or competitions.
- Event speakers preparing occasion-specific remarks.
- Community leaders, hosts, and organizers preparing public messages.
- Users who need reusable structures for recurring speaking contexts.
- Wedding speakers, ceremony speakers, and tribute speakers who need emotional tone without invented personal details.
- Non-native speakers who need clarity, tone, and pacing support while preserving their own voice.

## Core User Problems

- Speech drafts often lose the event, audience, and purpose context that shaped
  them.
- Users need separate drafts, templates, and revision notes without scattering
  them across documents.
- Speaking preparation needs readiness review, not only text editing.
- Reusable speech structures are valuable but should not become generic content
  templates for every writing task.
- AI-generated speech text can sound polished while missing authenticity,
  audience fit, or user ownership.
- Speech length and pacing need practical review because written word count does not always match delivery time.
- Users need help collecting real stories, facts, and constraints before drafting so the speech does not feel generic.

## Final Capabilities

- Create owner-scoped speech projects with occasion, event date, audience,
  tone, purpose, status, and notes.
- Create and edit speech drafts under projects.
- Store speaker, duration, key message, draft text, and readiness status.
- Save reusable speech templates tied to projects.
- Record history for drafting, editing, review, delivery, and status changes.
- Load list and dashboard screens with lightweight summaries.
- Load full editable text through detail workflows only when needed.
- Review speech readiness based on message clarity, audience fit, length, and
  delivery preparation.
- Export or hand off approved speeches to presentation or rehearsal workflows.
- Preserve user authorship and occasion context through every workflow.

## Advanced Capabilities

- AI-assisted outline generation from user-provided occasion context.
- Draft improvement suggestions with diff review.
- Delivery rehearsal notes and timing guidance.
- Export to markdown, PDF, or teleprompter-friendly plain text.
- Presentation Designer handoff for speech-to-slide support.
- Template library for recurring speech types.
- Version comparison across speech drafts.
- Outline-first workflows that let users review structure before full draft generation.
- Personal story and fact capture that remains user-authored and reviewable.
- Readiness checklist for audience, duration, call to action, and delivery.

## AI Opportunities

AI can assist with structure, clarity, tone, and rehearsal preparation, but it
must not replace the speaker's message or invent personal experiences.

Potential AI support includes:

- Suggesting outlines from occasion, audience, and purpose.
- Improving transitions or shortening long sections.
- Offering tone variants while preserving the user's intended message.
- Highlighting unclear key messages or unsupported claims.
- Estimating delivery length from draft text.
- Creating rehearsal prompts or speaker notes from approved draft content.

AI must not fabricate personal stories, credentials, facts, promises, audience
details, or organizational positions. AI speech output must remain a draft for
user review before use.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Presentation Designer can receive an approved outline or speaker notes.
- Grammar and Paraphrasing Assistant can improve wording without owning speech
  structure.
- Voice Converter can preview selected passages for delivery feel, with privacy
  review if text leaves the browser.
- Meeting Minutes AI may hand off approved meeting context for internal
  remarks.
- Proposal Writer or Research Assistant may provide source material for
  evidence-backed speeches.

Speech Writer owns speech projects, drafts, templates, and revision history. It
should not absorb slide design, voice synthesis, research collection, meeting
summaries, or general document editing.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return when preparing for events, revising remarks, reusing templates,
recording delivery history, or refining speech readiness before a presentation.

The weekly value is preparedness: keeping speaking work organized around real
occasions rather than scattered text drafts.

## Success Criteria

- Users can create projects, speeches, templates, and history records in a
  coherent speaking workflow.
- Occasion and audience context remain visible while drafting.
- Speech drafts are editable, reviewable, and easy to export or hand off.
- Readiness review improves preparation without promising delivery success.
- Speaking-time estimates and rehearsal notes are treated as guidance, not guarantees.
- AI assistance, if added, supports user voice and never invents identity or
  claims.
- Ecosystem handoffs enrich speech preparation without expanding product scope.

## Non Goals

- Do not become a generic document editor.
- Do not become a teleprompter platform by default.
- Do not become an event management system.
- Do not become a public speaking marketplace.
- Do not become a ghostwriting factory.
- Do not imitate living people, famous speakers, protected writer styles, or voices without explicit consent and approval.
- Do not add voice cloning, audio generation, or read-aloud identity features without separate governance.
- Do not invent personal stories, credentials, facts, or organizational claims.
- Do not replace Presentation Designer, Voice Converter, Research Assistant, or
  Grammar and Paraphrasing Assistant.
- Do not treat AI-generated speech text as user-approved content by default.

## Guiding Principles

- Start with occasion, audience, purpose, and key message.
- Preserve the user's voice and authorship.
- Treat templates as reusable structure, not generic content automation.
- Ground drafts in user-provided occasion, audience, stories, and facts.
- Keep revision history connected to speech activity.
- Make AI suggestions optional, reviewable, and grounded in user context.
- Support preparation without promising delivery outcomes.
- Use ecosystem handoffs instead of absorbing adjacent workflows.
