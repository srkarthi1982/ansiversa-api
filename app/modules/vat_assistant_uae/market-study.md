## Purpose

Study the official UAE VAT recordkeeping and filing environment so VAT Assistant UAE can remain useful while staying clearly outside official filing and professional tax advice.

## Problem Statement

UAE businesses need to track VAT registration identity, VAT return periods, output VAT, input VAT, taxable transactions, VAT rates, and filing status. Official obligations and filing remain with the Federal Tax Authority and EmaraTax, so a private workspace must focus on user-entered preparation records and clear boundaries.

## Target Users

- UAE small business owners.
- Finance administrators.
- Founders preparing records for accountants.
- Users coordinating VAT return information before external filing.

## Competitor Landscape

This V1 study is intentionally limited to official UAE references. The relevant alternative is not a commercial competitor but the official FTA/EmaraTax service environment where registration, filing, return submission, payment, TRN verification, and official service cards belong.

## Common Market Features

- VAT registration and TRN identity.
- VAT return submission and payment.
- VAT refund services.
- TRN verification.
- VAT calculator.
- Service cards and official e-services.
- Guides, public clarifications, FAQs, and legislation.

## User Love Signals

- Official services provide authority and final filing validity.
- TRN verification and VAT calculator tools reduce basic uncertainty.
- Public guides and service cards help users locate the right government workflow.

## Complaints And Friction

- Official services can feel procedural and filing-focused rather than preparation-focused.
- Businesses may still need a private place to organize notes, draft figures, transaction references, and adviser questions before entering official portals.
- Users can confuse planning estimates with official VAT return positions unless the product keeps boundaries visible.

## Pricing And Paywall Observations

Official FTA information pages, service listings, VAT calculator, TRN verification, and guidance surfaces are public or tied to government service access rather than a SaaS subscription model.

## AI Trends

AI could eventually summarize user-entered VAT records, flag missing fields, or explain where a record needs review. It must not provide VAT advice, determine obligations, or imply official acceptance.

## UX Patterns

- Keep official-source links visible.
- Separate registration identity, return periods, and transactions.
- Show clear filing and review statuses.
- Keep summaries transparent and based only on user-entered values.
- Repeat disclaimers around filing/advice boundaries.

## Opportunities

- Create a calm pre-filing preparation workspace.
- Help users organize records before accountant or tax-agent review.
- Provide deterministic summaries by VAT period and rate.
- Keep official filing boundaries explicit.
- Fit Ansiversa's one-shell mini-app ecosystem.

## Avoid List

- Do not copy official FTA wording, UI, service flows, or filing screens.
- Do not claim official filing support.
- Do not provide tax advice, eligibility determinations, or exemption guidance.
- Do not imply integration with FTA, EmaraTax, or UAEPass.
- Do not automate submissions or payments.

## Product Questions

## Review Notes

The FTA site identifies VAT, registration, refunds, guides, e-learning, EmaraTax, VAT Registration, Submit and Pay Tax Return, TRN verification, VAT calculator, service cards, legislation, and tax agents as official surfaces. VAT Assistant UAE should remain a private preparation layer that points users back to official and professional channels.
