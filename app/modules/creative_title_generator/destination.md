# Creative Title Generator Destination

## App Name

Creative Title Generator

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Creative Title Generator should become Ansiversa's focused title ideation
workspace: a place to save title briefs, generate multiple title directions,
compare options, and revisit previous generation runs without turning
Ansiversa into a full writing suite, SEO platform, ad optimization system, or
unreviewed AI content factory.

At maturity, Creative Title Generator should help users answer practical
questions like "What title angles fit this topic?", "Which option matches this
audience?", "What have I already tried?", and "Which title should I refine in
another writing tool?" The product should make title exploration repeatable by
keeping the brief, generated options, scores, and history connected.

The mature product should serve content creators, marketers, students,
founders, and teams that need fast title options for articles, videos,
products, social posts, emails, lessons, and internal content. It should remain
small enough to use quickly while preserving enough context for users to return
to an idea later.

## Target Users

- Content creators naming articles, videos, newsletters, and social posts.
- Marketing teams exploring campaign, product, and landing-page title angles.
- Solo founders writing product updates, launch copy, and feature headlines.
- Students and teachers naming projects, assignments, and learning material.
- Freelancers preparing title options for clients.
- Ansiversa users who need focused title ideation without a full writing suite.

## Core User Problems

- Users often need many title options before one feels right.
- One-off title ideas get scattered across notes, drafts, chat, and memory.
- Title quality depends on topic, audience, format, style, and keywords, but
  users rarely keep those inputs together.
- Generic generators can produce options without explainable context or review
  history.
- AI generation can create brand, accuracy, plagiarism, or quality concerns if
  outputs are not reviewable.
- Title ideation can drift into full content creation, SEO automation, ad
  testing, and publishing workflows if boundaries are unclear.

## Final Capabilities

- Create, edit, archive, and delete long-lived title projects.
- Store project topic, audience, language, status, and title brief context.
- Generate title options from a selected project with type, style, and optional
  keywords.
- Show generated titles with category, score, provider, and job context.
- Preserve immutable generated title records for review and comparison.
- Preserve generation job history so users can revisit what was generated and
  when.
- Keep dashboard and list responses lightweight with previews and counters.
- Provide detail views only where full project or title context is needed.
- Support favorite, shortlist, or selected-title workflows after review.
- Support AI provider-backed generation only after quality, privacy, cost, and
  governance review.
- Connect selected titles to adjacent writing apps through explicit handoffs.

## Advanced Capabilities

- Favorite and shortlist management for promising title options.
- Reusable title brief presets for recurring content types.
- A/B title variants and comparison notes.
- Tone, audience, and platform-specific title packs.
- Basic SEO and readability guidance without becoming an SEO platform.
- Export or copy packs for campaign review after governance review.
- AI provider-backed generation with visible provider, model, and review
  boundaries.
- Human review workflows for teams that approve titles before use.
- Versioned title projects so changed briefs remain explainable.

## AI Opportunities

- Generate stronger title options from topic, audience, style, and keywords.
- Suggest better title angles, formats, and audience framing.
- Explain why a title may be clearer, bolder, more playful, or more searchable.
- Identify duplicate, generic, misleading, or overpromising titles.
- Recommend variations for blogs, videos, products, social posts, and emails.
- Help users refine selected titles into adjacent writing workflows.
- Summarize generation history into practical next steps.

AI features must not bypass user review. Project topics, audiences, keywords,
generated titles, and history should be sent to an AI provider only through an
approved backend path with explicit governance, privacy handling, cost controls,
and clear product messaging.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Speech Writer: turn selected titles into speech or talk outlines through an
  explicit handoff.
- Social Caption Generator: use selected titles as caption hooks without
  absorbing social-caption workflow ownership.
- Prompt Builder: convert a title brief into a structured prompt after user
  action.
- Markdown Editor: export selected titles and notes into a planning document.
- Presentation Designer: use selected titles as slide, talk, or deck headings.
- Proposal Writer or Email Assistant: reuse selected titles only when the user
  intentionally moves them into those workflows.
- Dashboard or profile areas: may show high-level counts without exposing full
  project topics by default.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return weekly when preparing content calendars, campaigns, classes,
product updates, social posts, newsletters, or client drafts. The weekly value
is a repeatable ideation loop: create a brief, generate title directions,
compare options, save context, and return later without rebuilding the title
thinking from scratch.

The mature product earns trust by staying focused. It helps users create and
evaluate title options, but it does not publish content, run ad tests, claim
SEO guarantees, or replace human judgment.

## Success Criteria

- Users can create, edit, and reuse title briefs without confusion.
- Generated title options remain connected to their source project and job.
- Results are easy to scan, compare, and revisit.
- List and dashboard APIs stay lightweight while detail views provide required
  context.
- Users understand whether generation is deterministic, AI-backed, or otherwise
  provider-driven.
- Any AI provider, export, SEO guidance, or cross-app handoff is explicit and
  governance-reviewed.
- The product does not drift into a full writing suite, publishing platform,
  SEO automation product, ad optimization system, or content marketplace.
- Title ideation improves repeat creative work while preserving reviewability.

## Non Goals

Creative Title Generator is not intended to become:

- A full writing suite.
- A blog editor.
- A publishing platform.
- An SEO automation platform.
- An ad optimization or A/B testing service.
- A social media management tool.
- A content marketplace.
- A plagiarism checker.
- A brand compliance platform.
- An unreviewed AI content factory.

These directions should remain out of scope unless the destination itself is
reviewed and intentionally changed.

## Guiding Principles

Every Creative Title Generator feature should:

- Preserve the project-to-generation-to-review workflow.
- Keep title options connected to their brief and generation job.
- Improve title ideation without absorbing full writing workflows.
- Keep generated output reviewable and explainable.
- Use lightweight list responses and load full context only where needed.
- Treat AI generation as governed infrastructure, not a default shortcut.
- Keep SEO, export, team review, and cross-app handoffs explicit and scoped.
- Prefer focused handoffs to adjacent tools instead of absorbing their
  responsibilities.

## Governance Notes

This destination is aspirational. It describes the target product direction,
not the current implementation and not an authorization to build every feature
now.

destination.md is not a promise of what will be built next. It is a
description of what the product could ultimately become if time, user value,
and platform direction remain aligned.

Product owner and Astra review are required before accepting, prioritizing, or
implementing any destination item. Particular care is needed before approving
AI provider-backed generation, SEO guidance, export, publishing handoffs, team
review, A/B testing, or cross-app automation because title briefs can reveal
campaigns, product plans, client work, personal projects, internal strategy,
and unpublished content.
