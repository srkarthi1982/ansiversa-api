# Lesson Builder Destination

## App Name

Lesson Builder

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Lesson Builder should mature into a focused lesson-authoring workspace that
helps educators, tutors, trainers, and self-directed creators turn teaching
ideas into structured, reviewable, and reusable lesson plans.

The product should help users plan lessons with clear objectives, ordered
sections, activities, assessments, and resources without becoming an LMS,
classroom delivery platform, content marketplace, school administration system,
or collaborative document suite.

At its destination, Lesson Builder should make the path from lesson idea to
ready plan clear: draft the lesson, organize sections, review alignment, and
publish a stable version when ready.

## Target Users

- Teachers preparing classroom lessons.
- Tutors planning one-on-one or small-group sessions.
- Trainers designing workshops or professional learning sessions.
- Course creators organizing lesson plans before publishing elsewhere.
- Students or self-learners structuring material for peer teaching.
- New teachers, tutors, and homeschool parents who need structure without adopting a school platform.
- Educators adapting source material for different learner levels, language needs, or classroom constraints.

## Core User Problems

- Lesson ideas often lack a clear objective, sequence, or review point.
- Teaching activities can become disconnected from the intended learning
  outcome.
- Users need to revise drafts freely before locking a finished lesson.
- Published lesson plans should remain stable after review.
- Lesson planning can drift into LMS, classroom, or marketplace scope if
  boundaries are not explicit.
- AI-generated lesson drafts can look complete while still needing educator judgment for pacing, level, and teachability.
- Teachers need review checkpoints for objective clarity, activity fit, assessment evidence, materials, and timing realism.

## Final Capabilities

- Create owner-scoped lesson plans with subject, audience, duration, and
  objective.
- Add ordered lesson sections such as objective, instruction, activity,
  assessment, and resource.
- Edit lesson details and section content while the lesson remains a draft.
- Reorder lesson sections to shape the teaching flow.
- Publish a lesson when the structure is ready.
- Lock published lessons so the approved plan remains stable.
- Support versioned publishing if future editing after publish is required.
- Review lesson alignment between objective, activities, and assessment.
- Export finished lesson plans for teaching or sharing.
- Connect selected concepts, research, or course modules through explicit
  handoffs.

## Advanced Capabilities

- Lesson templates for common teaching formats.
- Versioned publishing for updates after initial publish.
- Export to PDF, markdown, or presentation-ready outline.
- Concept Explainer handoff for difficult topics.
- Research Assistant handoff for source-backed lesson material.
- Course Tracker connection for course/module placement.
- AI-assisted lesson outline suggestions from user-provided objectives.
- Differentiation controls for reading level, learner profile, and activity type after governance review.
- Section-level review for source assumptions, learner fit, assessment evidence, and timing.
- Accessibility and timing checks for lesson sections.

## AI Opportunities

AI may help draft lesson structures and review alignment, but it must not
replace educator judgment or claim instructional authority.

Potential AI support includes:

- Suggesting a section sequence from a user-provided objective.
- Drafting activity ideas for a target audience.
- Checking whether assessment sections match the stated objective.
- Suggesting timing adjustments for long or short lessons.
- Turning reviewed concepts into lesson sections.
- Identifying missing resources or unclear instructions.

AI must not publish automatically, replace teacher review, guarantee learning
outcomes, generate unsafe instructional guidance, or use copyrighted material
without explicit user-provided context and governance.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Concept Explainer can provide reviewed explanations for lesson sections.
- Research Assistant can provide source-backed material for lesson preparation.
- Course Tracker can connect lessons to course modules.
- Presentation Designer can receive a published lesson outline for slides.
- AI Notes Summarizer can provide reviewed notes for lesson planning.
- Smart Textbook Scanner can hand off reviewed textbook notes where permitted.

Lesson Builder owns lesson authoring and publish state. It should not absorb
course management, presentation design, research collection, concept
explanation, or classroom delivery.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return when preparing new lessons, revising drafts, reusing lesson
structures, publishing stable plans, or adapting reviewed material for a
different audience.

The weekly value is preparation quality: turning teaching intent into a
structured plan that can be delivered or handed off.

## Success Criteria

- Users can create, edit, reorder, review, and publish lesson plans in one
  focused workflow.
- Draft lessons remain flexible while published lessons remain stable.
- Sections support clear objective, instruction, activity, assessment, and
  resource planning.
- Generated or adapted lesson material remains editable, source-aware, and educator-reviewed before use.
- Future AI assistance supports educator judgment and never bypasses review.
- Ecosystem handoffs improve lesson preparation without turning the app into an
  LMS or presentation tool.
- The product remains useful for manual lesson planning.

## Non Goals

- Do not become an LMS.
- Do not become a classroom delivery platform.
- Do not become a school administration system.
- Do not become a content marketplace.
- Do not become a collaborative document editor.
- Do not replace Presentation Designer, Course Tracker, Research Assistant, or
  Concept Explainer.
- Do not auto-publish AI-generated lessons.
- Do not imply standards alignment, classroom readiness, or differentiation accuracy without review.
- Do not collect student data or classroom context without approved privacy governance.
- Do not guarantee learning outcomes.
- Do not use copyrighted source material without explicit user context and
  governance review.

## Guiding Principles

- Start with the learning objective.
- Keep lesson flow ordered, reviewable, and easy to revise.
- Protect published lesson stability.
- Support educator judgment rather than replacing it.
- Treat lessons as teaching decisions, not just generated content.
- Use ecosystem handoffs to enrich lessons without absorbing other apps.
- Make AI suggestions optional, explainable, and editable.
- Prefer practical teaching preparation over platform expansion.
