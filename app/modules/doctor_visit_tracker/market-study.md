## Purpose

This study frames Doctor Visit Tracker as a personal appointment and visit-history organizer. It is not a medical decision-support product.

## Problem Statement

People often lose track of appointment dates, doctor names, clinics, reasons for visits, follow-up dates, medications mentioned during visits, and out-of-pocket costs. Notes may be scattered across paper, chat messages, calendars, and memory.

## Target Users

Individuals, parents, caregivers, and family organizers who need a private record of scheduled and completed doctor visits.

## Competitor Landscape

Calendar apps support appointment reminders. Note apps support free-form medical notes. Health portals store provider records but are fragmented by clinic. Personal health record tools can be heavy or region-specific.

## User Pain Points

Missed follow-ups, difficulty remembering previous doctor context, unclear expense history, and weak search across clinics and specialties.

## Ansiversa Differentiation

A focused owner-scoped mini-app can combine appointment metadata, personal notes, specialties, follow-ups, and cost summaries without becoming a clinical tool.

## Privacy Considerations

Doctor visit records can be sensitive. All rows must be authenticated and owner-scoped. No external healthcare integration is included in V1.

## Safety Considerations

The app must not diagnose, recommend treatment, prescribe medication, replace professional advice, or act as an emergency medical system.

## Scope Boundaries

No AI diagnosis, treatment recommendations, medication guidance, emergency support, provider integrations, or medical record exchange.
