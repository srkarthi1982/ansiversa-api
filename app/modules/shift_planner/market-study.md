# Shift Planner Market Study

## Document Status
Research complete for the Level 3 planning workflow. Market version 1.0; created and reviewed 2026-07-16; next review 2027-01-16.

## Purpose and problem
Small teams often need a simpler alternative to enterprise workforce suites: reusable shift patterns, a lightweight roster, conflict warnings, and an hours overview without payroll or HR administration.

## Target users and landscape
Individuals, family businesses, shops, restaurants, and informal service teams are the primary users. Products such as [Homebase](https://joinhomebase.com/), [Sling](https://getsling.com/), and [When I Work](https://wheniwork.com/) demonstrate demand for schedule visibility, reusable shifts, conflict prevention, and mobile-friendly workflows. Their broad workforce, messaging, time-clock, and payroll features also show where a focused planner can remain simpler.

## Common signals
Users value fast schedule creation, clear assignees, overnight support, conflict detection, and useful mobile layouts. Friction commonly comes from dense calendars, paywalls around basic scheduling, notification noise, and coupling with payroll. AI-generated schedules are an emerging market feature, but are not required for this product.

## Ansiversa opportunity and avoid list
Offer a calm record-first planner with types, members, shifts, filters, and accurate net hours. Avoid payroll, wages, attendance claims, biometric data, labor-law calculations, external messaging, calendar integrations, and AI scheduling.

## Product questions and review notes
Future research may assess recurring shifts and a lightweight weekly view. Neither is committed. This study informs `destination.md` but does not define scope. All summaries are original and no competitor UI or proprietary workflow is copied.
