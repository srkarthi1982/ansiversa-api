## App Summary

Quiz is an Ansiversa learning app that helps authenticated users practice knowledge through secure, server-graded quiz attempts. Users choose taxonomy filters such as platform, subject, topic, roadmap, and level, start an attempt, submit answers once, and review their results with selected answers, correct answers, and explanations.

The current product is built for calm, trustworthy practice rather than noisy competition. It gives students, teachers, parents, self-learners, and trainers a reliable way to check understanding, revisit completed attempts, and turn learning into measurable progress.

## Core Value Proposition

Traditional studying often feels productive without proving whether the learner can recall or apply the material. Traditional quizzes can be slow to create, hard to grade, or too focused on speed and entertainment. Quiz gives users a focused practice loop: select a topic, complete a server-owned attempt, receive authoritative scoring, review mistakes with explanations, and revisit results later.

The product's strongest value is trust. The browser does not control scoring, answer keys are withheld during play, attempts preserve the exact question set, and completed results can be reviewed after submission. This makes Quiz useful for everyday practice today and a strong foundation for future analytics, weak-area recommendations, teacher workflows, and AI-assisted question creation.

## Problems We Solve

- Students study for long periods but do not always know what they can actually recall.
- Learners receive scores without enough explanation to understand mistakes.
- Teachers and trainers need repeatable knowledge checks without manual grading every time.
- Parents need simple progress signals that are more meaningful than "I studied."
- Self-learners need lightweight practice without adopting a full learning management system.
- Users need a quiz flow where answer keys are protected until submission or review.
- Learners need attempt history so practice does not disappear after one session.
- Educational tools can become too noisy, competitive, or speed-focused for careful review.
- General AI tools can draft questions, but they do not automatically provide trusted hosting, scoring, result history, and answer-key handling.

## Target Audience

### Students

Students use Quiz to check readiness before school tests, college assessments, certifications, and exam practice. They need fast practice, clear scoring, and explanations that help them understand why an answer was wrong.

### Teachers

Teachers use Quiz as a formative check to see whether learners understand a topic. They value reusable practice flows, reliable scoring, and future analytics that can identify difficult questions or weak topics.

### Parents

Parents use Quiz to support home learning with visible progress signals. They need simple results, clear explanations, and confidence that practice time is producing measurable improvement.

### Educational Institutions

Schools, colleges, tutoring centers, and training organizations can use Quiz as part of a broader digital learning ecosystem. They need trust, consistency, protected answer handling, mobile access, and future pathways for reporting and assignment workflows.

### Self Learners

Self learners use Quiz to test memory after reading, watching lessons, taking notes, or studying independently. They need low-friction practice and result history without the overhead of a full classroom system.

### Coaching Centres

Coaching centres need structured practice across subjects, topics, levels, and exam preparation pathways. Quiz can support repeatable practice now and, in future versions, richer progress summaries and weak-area drills.

### Corporate Trainers

Corporate trainers need knowledge checks for onboarding, workshops, compliance, and skill refreshers. They value secure attempts, authoritative scoring, and future support for private quiz banks and cohort reporting.

## Visual Style Guide

### Colours

- Use the broader Ansiversa brand palette where available.
- Favor clean learning colors: crisp white or soft neutral surfaces, deep readable text, confident blue for trust, fresh green for progress, and restrained amber for review or attention states.
- Avoid overly playful neon palettes, heavy red failure states, or competition-first visuals.
- Use color to clarify status: selected, correct, incorrect, explanation, completed, and progress.

### Lighting

- Natural, bright, study-friendly lighting.
- Soft daylight for home and student scenes.
- Clean classroom or training-room lighting for teacher and corporate scenes.
- Avoid dark, dramatic, or high-pressure exam-room lighting unless showing the problem before Quiz enters.

### Transitions

- Smooth, purposeful transitions between study, attempt, submit, and review.
- Use gentle screen wipes, focus pulls, and UI zooms.
- Avoid chaotic cuts, exaggerated game effects, confetti-heavy moments, or noisy competitive energy.

### Pacing

- Calm but active.
- Start with uncertainty, move quickly into practice, then spend meaningful time on review and learning.
- The 15-second version should feel crisp.
- The 30-second version should balance audience breadth and product clarity.
- The 60-second version should feel premium, human, and globally relevant.

### Cinematic Style

- Modern educational technology commercial.
- Human-centered, realistic, and trustworthy.
- Show actual product-like screens whenever possible.
- Use close-ups of thoughtful learners, teachers preparing, parents supporting, and trainers reviewing.
- Keep interface overlays readable and uncluttered.
- Do not imply features that are not current, such as live multiplayer, public leaderboards, adaptive AI, or teacher authoring, unless placed in future-facing concept scenes.
