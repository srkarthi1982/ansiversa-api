# Quiz Destination

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Quiz should become a full learning and knowledge-check platform inside
Ansiversa: a place where users can practice, diagnose weak areas, build
confidence, and convert learning material into measurable progress.

At maturity, Quiz should support structured practice for students, teachers,
self-learners, parents, trainers, and knowledge workers. It should help users
answer three practical questions: What do I know? What am I missing? What
should I practice next?

The final product should feel useful both for a five-minute practice session
and for a longer learning plan across subjects, courses, exams, work topics,
and personal knowledge goals.

## Target Users

- Students preparing for school, college, certifications, and exams.
- Teachers who want reusable practice sets and review signals.
- Self-learners building knowledge across topics.
- Parents supporting a child's weekly practice.
- Trainers creating checks for workshops, onboarding, or compliance.
- Knowledge workers testing retention after reading, meetings, or research.

## Core User Problems

- Users often study passively and do not know whether they can recall material.
- Learners need fast feedback with explanations, not just scores.
- Teachers and trainers need repeatable checks without rebuilding quizzes from
  scratch every time.
- Parents need simple progress signals instead of vague "studied today" claims.
- Self-learners need to identify weak areas and revisit them later.
- Knowledge workers need lightweight ways to retain notes, concepts, and
  course material over time.

## Final Capabilities

- Browse and filter quiz taxonomy by platform, subject, topic, roadmap, and
  difficulty.
- Start secure server-owned attempts with stable question sets.
- Submit answers once and receive authoritative scoring.
- Review each question with selected answer, correct answer, explanation, and
  weak-area tags.
- View attempt history, score trends, time spent, accuracy by topic, and recent
  mistakes.
- Resume unfinished attempts before expiry where appropriate.
- Save favorite quizzes, topics, and practice filters.
- Support teacher/trainer-created quiz sets with draft, review, publish, and
  reuse flows.
- Support parent or mentor views for progress summaries where permissions
  allow.
- Offer practice modes such as quick practice, timed test, review mistakes,
  weak-area drill, and roadmap checkpoint.
- Track mastery by topic and recommend what to practice next.
- Export or share safe progress summaries without exposing answer keys.
- Keep quiz results connected to the broader Ansiversa learning ecosystem.

## Advanced Capabilities

- Adaptive difficulty that changes question selection based on recent
  performance.
- Spaced repetition queues for missed or uncertain questions.
- Classroom or cohort assignment workflows.
- Private quiz banks for teachers, companies, or families.
- Exam simulation mode with sections, timing, pass thresholds, and analytics.
- Challenge mode for friendly competitions without public leaderboards by
  default.
- Offline-friendly mobile practice later, with secure sync when the user
  returns online.
- Rich analytics dashboards for teachers and trainers.
- Versioned question banks so results remain explainable after questions are
  edited.

## AI Opportunities

- Generate draft questions from notes, textbook excerpts, course outlines, or
  Concept Explainer content.
- Suggest answer options, distractors, explanations, and difficulty levels.
- Detect weak areas from attempt history and recommend targeted practice.
- Personalize the next attempt based on confidence, mistakes, speed, and topic
  coverage.
- Explain wrong answers in simpler language or with alternate examples.
- Convert missed questions into flashcards, memory drills, or study tasks.
- Flag ambiguous or low-quality questions for human review.
- Summarize weekly learning progress for students, parents, teachers, and
  trainers.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Study Planner: turn weak quiz areas into scheduled study tasks and review
  sessions.
- Course Tracker: attach quizzes to course modules and use scores as completion
  signals.
- Concept Explainer: launch concept explanations from missed questions or
  generate quiz checks from explained concepts.
- Formula Finder: connect math and science questions to relevant formula
  references and practice sets.
- AI Notes Summarizer: generate quiz drafts from saved summaries and key
  points.
- Daily Word Challenge: reuse vocabulary practice patterns and streak signals
  for language-focused quizzes.
- Memory Trainer: send missed facts or repeated mistakes into memory drills and
  spaced repetition practice.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return every week because Quiz gives them a clear practice loop: take a
short attempt, see what changed, review mistakes, and know what to practice
next. Teachers and parents return for progress signals. Self-learners return
because the app turns scattered study into measurable improvement.

The mature product should make weekly improvement visible without requiring the
user to build a learning system from scratch.

## Success Criteria

- Users can start, complete, review, and revisit quiz attempts without confusion.
- Scores, explanations, and history are trusted because grading is server-owned.
- Users can identify weak topics within a few minutes of practice.
- Weekly practice produces visible progress trends.
- Teachers or trainers can create and reuse quiz sets without developer help.
- AI-generated content is clearly reviewable and does not bypass human quality
  control.
- Quiz data feeds useful signals into Study Planner, Course Tracker, Concept
  Explainer, AI Notes Summarizer, Daily Word Challenge, Memory Trainer, and
  other learning apps.
- The app remains fast, mobile-friendly, accessible, and safe for answer-key
  handling.

## Non Goals

Quiz is not intended to become:

- A social network.
- A chat platform.
- A gambling or betting game.
- A public competition system that prioritizes status over learning.
- A generic content publishing platform unrelated to learning assessment.

These directions should stay out of scope unless the destination itself is
reviewed and intentionally changed.

## Guiding Principles

Every Quiz feature should:

- Improve learning.
- Solve a real user problem.
- Reduce friction.
- Preserve trust in scoring, answer handling, and explanations.
- Encourage repeat practice without unhealthy pressure.
- Fit naturally into the Ansiversa learning ecosystem.
- Stay useful on mobile.

## Governance Notes

This destination is aspirational. It describes the target product direction,
not the current implementation and not an authorization to build every feature
now.

destination.md is not a promise of what will be built next. It is a
description of what the product could ultimately become if time, user value,
and platform direction remain aligned.

Product owner and Astra review are required before accepting, prioritizing, or
implementing any destination item. Feature bloat should be removed during
review. Real user value, ecosystem alignment, safe answer handling, and
maintainable backend ownership should guide every future Quiz decision.
