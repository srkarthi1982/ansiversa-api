## App Summary

Meal Planner is an Ansiversa lifestyle app that helps authenticated users save reusable recipes, create weekly meal plans, and schedule breakfast, lunch, dinner, or snack entries on a calendar. The current product is Workflow Ready and focused on user-authored planning, not diet, medical, allergy, nutrition, grocery, shopping, or AI recommendations.

The product helps individuals and households reduce everyday meal decision fatigue by keeping recipes, plans, and dated meal entries in one practical workspace.

## Brand Identity

Meal Planner belongs to the Ansiversa ecosystem. Every marketing asset should feel simple, trustworthy, intelligent, professional, accessible, and globally understandable.

The brand identity should communicate:

- Simplicity: weekly meal planning should feel easier to maintain.
- Trust: food planning data should be user-owned and private.
- Intelligence: the product should support planning structure without becoming prescriptive.
- Professionalism: useful for individuals, households, students, and busy professionals.
- Accessibility: meals and planning language should feel global and flexible.
- Everyday calm: the emotional center is less decision fatigue.

Avoid:

- Medical, diet, weight-loss, allergy, or nutrition authority claims.
- Perfect-family or unrealistic cooking imagery.
- AI meal generation claims before approval.
- Grocery, pantry, shopping, or recipe import claims as current features.
- Prescriptive language about what users should eat.

## Core Value Proposition

Meal planning often breaks because recipes, schedules, notes, and household decisions live in separate places. Meal Planner gives users a focused workspace for reusable recipes, weekly plans, and calendar entries, while allowing simple custom meal titles when a full recipe is unnecessary.

Its strongest value is flexibility. It supports practical planning without becoming a diet authority or rigid nutrition system. Users can plan around real routines, changing schedules, and everyday meals.

## Problems We Solve

- Users face repeated last-minute meal decisions.
- Recipes and weekly plans live in separate tools or notes.
- Households need flexible meals, not rigid diet plans.
- Planning apps can require too much setup before becoming useful.
- Not every meal needs a full recipe record.
- Users need dated entries across breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks.
- Food planning can drift into health claims without clear boundaries.
- Future grocery, AI, nutrition, and wellness features need careful governance.

## Marketing Guardrails

- Do not claim grocery lists, pantry tracking, recipe import, nutrition summaries, reminders, collaboration, exports, AI suggestions, or cross-app recommendations as current features.
- Do not present Meal Planner as a diet, medical, allergy, nutrition, or weight-loss authority.
- Do not imply professional health advice.
- Do not require every meal to look like a complete recipe.
- Do not copy competitor recipes, meal templates, grocery grouping logic, screenshots, or proprietary flows.
- Keep the central promise simple: save recipes, create meal plans, schedule meal entries, and reduce everyday decision fatigue.
