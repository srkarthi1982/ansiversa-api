## Purpose

Symptom Journal studies the need for private personal symptom record keeping. The app is not a diagnostic, triage, treatment, medication, emergency, or clinical accuracy product.

## Problem Statement

People often forget when symptoms happened, how severe they felt, which body area was affected, or what context may have mattered. A structured journal helps users remember observations before speaking with qualified healthcare professionals.

## Target Users

- Adults who want a private symptom history.
- Caregivers who keep observation notes for family members.
- Users preparing for medical appointments.

## Competitor Landscape

Common alternatives include paper notebooks, notes apps, spreadsheet logs, personal health trackers, and symptom-specific mobile apps. Many market tools add reminders, charts, medication logs, AI summaries, or condition-oriented language. Ansiversa should keep V1 simpler and safer.

## Common Market Features

- Dated symptom entries.
- Severity scales.
- Body area and mood fields.
- Trigger and relief notes.
- History charts and recent activity.
- Search and filters.

## User Love Signals

Users value quick entry, privacy, searchable history, exportable notes, and simple summaries they can review before appointments.

## Complaints And Friction

Users dislike diagnostic claims, excessive forms, confusing medical language, intrusive reminders, ads around sensitive data, and unclear privacy boundaries.

## Pricing And Paywall Observations

Symptom tracking often appears as a free basic feature with paid analytics, sharing, export, or advanced reports. Ansiversa V1 should remain within the platform's free coming-soon workflow until approval.

## AI Trends

Some products use AI to summarize symptoms or suggest possible causes. Symptom Journal V1 intentionally excludes AI, diagnosis, disease prediction, and treatment recommendations.

## UX Patterns

Good experiences prioritize fast entry, plain language, severity controls, clear filters, and non-alarming summaries.

## Opportunities

- Calm personal health observation workflow.
- Clear safety boundaries.
- Practical summaries without medical conclusions.
- Strong owner-scoped privacy discipline.

## Avoid List

- Diagnosis.
- Disease prediction.
- Medication or treatment advice.
- Emergency guidance.
- Clinical accuracy claims.
- Healthcare integrations in V1.

## Sources
