## Purpose

Errand Planner studies how users organize everyday outside-the-home tasks such as groceries, pharmacy visits, banking, school pickups, courier trips, government office visits, home supplies, vehicle errands, and business errands.

## Problem Statement

General task apps can manage errands, but they often mix work tasks, projects, reminders, collaboration, and calendar workflows. Users who simply need to remember what must be done, where it must happen, how urgent it is, and whether it is finished benefit from a smaller errand-specific workspace.

## Target Users

- Individuals managing weekly personal errands.
- Families coordinating household trips without a full project-management tool.
- Small-business owners tracking local office, courier, bank, purchase, and government errands.
- Caregivers or household organizers who need clear due dates and notes.

## Competitor Landscape

- Todoist: broad task management with due dates, labels, priorities, comments, reminders, and calendar integrations. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todoist
- Any.do: productivity platform spanning tasks, reminders, calendar, grocery list, assistants, family and workspace tiers. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Any.do
- Google Tasks: simple personal tasks integrated into Google Workspace and Calendar surfaces. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Tasks
- Microsoft To Do: list-based personal task management within the Microsoft ecosystem. Source: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/todo
- Apple Reminders: ecosystem-native reminders, lists, dates, locations, subtasks, and shared lists. Source: https://support.apple.com/guide/reminders/welcome/icloud

## Common Market Features

- Task title, notes, due date, reminder, and completion state.
- Lists, labels, categories, folders, projects, or sections.
- Priorities and today/upcoming views.
- Calendar integration or reminder notifications.
- Search and simple filtering.
- Mobile-first capture.
- Collaboration or sharing in broader products.

## User Love Signals

- Fast capture matters more than heavy setup.
- Today, upcoming, and overdue views reduce mental load.
- Categories or labels are useful when they stay simple.
- Clean completion and archive behavior helps users trust the list.
- Cross-device access is expected in mature task products.

## Complaints And Friction

- Full task managers can feel too complex for errands.
- Reminder and calendar features can create notification fatigue.
- Collaboration, teams, projects, and AI assistants can distract from a short personal list.
- Some simple products lack strong filtering, priority, or context fields.
- Paywalls often sit around reminders, calendar views, sharing, or AI.

## Pricing And Paywall Observations

Freemium products commonly keep basic task capture free while charging for advanced reminders, labels, collaboration, calendar views, upload limits, or AI assistance. Errand Planner V1 should remain a focused private workspace and avoid implying paid automation, navigation, or integrations that do not exist.

## AI Trends

AI task products increasingly suggest subtasks, summaries, or automation. For errands, AI may eventually help group errands or suggest missing checklist details, but V1 must not claim AI planning, route optimization, or external service automation.

## UX Patterns Worth Studying

- Today/upcoming/overdue summaries.
- Compact list rows with status, due date, priority, and category.
- Fast edit drawers.
- Clear archived/completed separation.
- Simple category setup with color hints.

## Ansiversa Opportunities

- Keep errands separate from heavy project management.
- Support private, owner-scoped records with minimal fields.
- Show due-today, due-soon, overdue, completed, and archived metrics.
- Preserve the platform shell and one-login experience.
- Use shared drawers, record actions, pagination, and generated API contracts.

## Avoid List

- Do not add GPS navigation, maps, route optimization, delivery tracking, or calendar replacement behavior.
- Do not claim reminders or push notifications before that platform capability exists.
- Do not add AI assistant claims.
- Do not copy competitor wording, templates, UI, or scoring systems.

## Product Questions

- Should V2 include reminder notifications through a shared Ansiversa notification service?
- Should categories remain user-managed only, or should default category suggestions be seeded?
- Should errands support recurring schedules, or remain one-off records?
- Should household sharing be handled here or remain outside V1?
