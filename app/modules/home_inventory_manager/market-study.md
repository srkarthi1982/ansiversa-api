## Purpose

This study captures public market patterns around home inventory and belongings organizers. It informs Ansiversa product thinking but does not define implementation commitments.

## Problem Statement

People own household belongings across rooms, boxes, closets, and storage spaces. They often remember important items only when moving, decluttering, reviewing warranties, or recovering from damage. A lightweight inventory helps users record what they own, where it is, what it may be worth, and whether warranty dates need attention.

## Target Users

- Homeowners and renters tracking furniture, electronics, appliances, tools, and valuables.
- Families organizing belongings by room and category.
- People preparing for moves, maintenance, warranty review, or decluttering.
- Everyday planners who want a practical list of household items without business inventory complexity.

## Competitor Landscape

- NAIC Home Inventory focuses on documenting belongings for possible insurance claims and includes barcode scanning, photos, grouping, and export guidance.
- Sortly positions itself as a broad inventory platform with folders, locations, barcode/QR workflows, reporting, and team/business usage.
- Encircle is oriented toward property restoration and contents documentation, with room/box organization, photos, and report generation.
- Newer inventory tools increasingly emphasize AI photo recognition, valuation, and automated item extraction, which are outside Ansiversa V1.
- General spreadsheets and notes apps remain common alternatives because they are simple, flexible, and familiar.

## Common Market Features

- Item title, category, room/location, quantity, value, condition, and notes.
- Purchase date, warranty date, brand, model, and serial number fields.
- Photo, receipt, barcode, and report/export features in fuller products.
- Search and filters by room, category, condition, warranty, and value.
- Summary dashboards for total items, value, locations, and recent additions.

## User Love Signals

- Users value fast entry and low ceremony more than enterprise-grade inventory controls.
- Room and category filters match how people remember belongings.
- Warranty and serial number fields are useful for electronics and appliances.
- Value summaries help orient users without turning the app into accounting.
- Simple exports and reports are useful later, but not essential to a focused V1.

## Complaints And Friction

- Business inventory products can feel too heavy for casual home use.
- Photo and barcode workflows may be useful but can make initial setup slower.
- Insurance-focused apps may imply claims or policy integration that users do not always need.
- AI valuation and recognition raise privacy and accuracy questions.
- Large item catalogs become hard to review if search and filters are weak.

## Pricing And Paywall Observations

- Business tools commonly monetize advanced reporting, barcode/QR labels, team access, and higher item limits.
- Consumer home inventory products may be free when tied to insurance education or paid when adding sync, export, and media storage.
- Ansiversa V1 should avoid storage-heavy media and premium claims, keeping the workflow useful as a lightweight owner-scoped record system.

## AI Trends

- AI photo recognition, automatic item extraction, and value estimation are visible trends.
- These trends may help future versions, but V1 intentionally excludes AI to protect privacy, avoid inaccurate valuation, and keep the product lightweight.

## UX Patterns Worth Studying

- Item-first list with quick create/edit drawers.
- Room, category, condition, warranty, and archive filters near the item list.
- Category counts that reveal where inventory records are concentrated.
- Dashboard cards for total items, archived items, warranty attention, and estimated value.
- Recent additions and highest-value lists for quick review.

## Ansiversa Opportunities

- Provide a focused home belongings inventory inside the existing Ansiversa shell.
- Avoid ecommerce, ERP, warehouse, accounting, insurance, barcode, smart-home, and AI scope.
- Keep V1 centered on owner-scoped categories, items, archive/restore, warranty attention, and insights.
- Pair naturally with future Home & Family apps without becoming a general asset-management platform.

## Avoid List

- Do not copy competitor wording, screenshots, workflows, reports, or templates.
- Do not imply insurance claim preparation, policy integration, appraisal, or official valuation.
- Do not add photos, receipts, attachments, barcode scanning, QR labels, smart-home sync, or AI in V1.
- Do not model warehouses, SKUs, stock movement, ecommerce, or ERP workflows.

## Product Questions

- Should V2 support CSV import/export after the base workflow is reviewed?
- Should future versions allow photos or receipts, and where would storage governance live?
- Should estimated value stay user-entered only, or should future valuation helpers be considered?
- Should warranty reminders stay inside this app or connect to a shared reminders system later?
