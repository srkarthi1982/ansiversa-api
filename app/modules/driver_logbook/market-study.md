## Purpose

Understand the personal driving log and mileage journal market enough to build a focused Ansiversa V1 without copying competitor workflows or making official compliance claims.

## Problem Statement

Drivers often need a simple memory aid for where they drove, why they drove, which vehicle they used, and how much distance was covered. Many existing tools are either too compliance-heavy, too fleet-oriented, or dependent on GPS automation that some users may not want.

## Target Users

- Individuals who want a personal trip history.
- Households tracking vehicle use.
- Freelancers who want organized records before preparing separate official documentation.
- Drivers who prefer manual odometer records over location tracking.

## Competitor Landscape

The market includes mileage trackers, fleet logbooks, tax-oriented mileage tools, spreadsheet templates, and GPS trip-detection apps. Many products optimize for tax deductions, employer reimbursement, or commercial fleet oversight.

## Common Market Features

- Vehicle records.
- Trip date and purpose.
- Start and destination fields.
- Odometer or GPS-based distance.
- Monthly and yearly distance summaries.
- Export or printable reports.

## User Love Signals

Users value quick entry, automatic distance calculation, clean history search, and summaries that make old trips easy to understand.

## Complaints And Friction

Common friction includes subscription pressure, privacy concerns around location tracking, complicated compliance language, and manual cleanup of auto-detected trips.

## Pricing And Paywall Observations

Many mileage apps reserve exports, tax reports, or multi-vehicle features for paid plans. A lightweight personal record can be valuable even without those advanced capabilities.

## AI Trends

AI is sometimes used for receipt parsing or trip categorization, but Driver Logbook V1 should remain deterministic and manual-entry only.

## UX Patterns

Useful patterns include a fast trip form, clear vehicle selector, purpose chips, date filters, and summary cards by month, vehicle, and purpose.

## Opportunities

Ansiversa can offer a calm personal logbook that does not require GPS access, does not overwhelm users with fleet tools, and clearly avoids official compliance promises.

## Avoid List

- Do not imply legal certification.
- Do not calculate commercial driver hours.
- Do not introduce GPS tracking.
- Do not copy tax-reporting flows.
- Do not claim official logbook replacement.

## Product Questions

- Should a future version support CSV export after manual approval?
- Should printable personal summaries be added?
- Should trip templates be supported for repeated routes?
