## Purpose

This market study captures the public context around UAE corporate tax preparation, record organization, and estimate review. It is research only. It does not define tax advice, filing logic, legal interpretation, or product commitments.

## Problem Statement

UAE businesses need a practical place to organize financial-year periods, adjustment notes, filing-related tasks, and adviser-review preparation. Official obligations and interpretations remain with the UAE Ministry of Finance, the Federal Tax Authority, legislation, and qualified professionals, but many small teams still need a private workspace before external filing or professional review.

## Target Users

- UAE small-business owners tracking corporate-tax preparation records.
- Finance/admin teams organizing periods, adjustments, and supporting notes.
- Founders preparing questions and schedules for an accountant or tax adviser.
- Businesses that need deadline visibility without claiming automated compliance.

## Competitor Landscape

- Official government channels: Ministry of Finance and Federal Tax Authority pages provide law, guidance, registration, filing, and service information. They are authoritative but are not personal planning workspaces.
- Professional tax/accounting software: enterprise or accounting packages can manage ledgers, filings, and compliance workflows, often requiring setup, accounting knowledge, or adviser involvement.
- General bookkeeping tools: expense and accounting tools help capture records, but may not expose UAE corporate-tax assumption tracking as a simple mini-app workflow.
- Spreadsheets and shared documents: flexible and common, but easy to fragment, mislabel, or mix currencies and assumptions without a consistent audit trail.

## Common Market Features

- Registration and filing information.
- Financial-year setup.
- Profit and taxable-income calculations.
- Adjustment schedules.
- Task and deadline tracking.
- Document collection.
- Adviser or accountant handoff.
- Exportable summaries.

## User Love Signals

- Clear deadline visibility.
- Plain-language task organization.
- Easy handoff to accountants.
- Transparent calculation basis.
- Ability to separate draft assumptions from externally confirmed treatments.

## Complaints and Friction

- Users can confuse estimates with official liability.
- Tax rules, thresholds, elections, exemptions, and deadlines can change.
- Spreadsheets silently mix assumptions or currencies.
- Professional software can feel heavy for simple preparation tracking.
- Users need authoritative references, not social-media interpretations.

## Pricing and Paywall Observations

Professional accounting/tax systems usually sit behind business software subscriptions or adviser services. Government filing services are official channels rather than productivity workspaces. Ansiversa V1 should remain a private planning workspace and avoid competing with regulated filing/advisory services.

## AI Trends

AI is increasingly used for document summarization and workflow assistance, but tax-related outputs carry high compliance risk. Any future AI features should remain bounded to organizing user-entered data, drafting questions for advisers, or summarizing uploaded records with clear verification requirements.

## UX Patterns

- Period-first setup.
- Adjustment tables grouped by category and direction.
- Status labels for draft, review, and externally confirmed items.
- Deadline sections for upcoming and overdue tasks.
- Calculation footnotes and assumption panels.
- Export-friendly summaries.

## Ansiversa Opportunities

- Offer a lightweight planning workspace inside the existing protected shell.
- Keep calculations transparent and estimate-only.
- Group totals by currency to avoid hidden conversions.
- Make adviser-review status visible on each adjustment.
- Track obligations without asserting legal applicability.

## Avoid List

- Do not provide legal, accounting, audit, filing, or tax advice.
- Do not imply FTA approval or filing acceptance.
- Do not claim direct Federal Tax Authority integration.
- Do not automatically determine eligibility, exemptions, elections, or obligations.
- Do not silently mix currencies or hide rate/threshold assumptions.

## Product Questions

- Should future versions support document attachments and exportable schedules?
- Should rate and threshold assumptions be managed globally per user?
- Should multiple entities share common adviser contacts?
- Should audit-trail history be added before export workflows?
