## App Summary

Client Feedback Analyzer is an Ansiversa business app that helps users organize client profiles, feedback records, actionable insights, and review reports. The current live V1 stores manual analysis records with owner-scoped access, lightweight lists, and detail endpoints.

The product is built for evidence-backed feedback review. It is not a CRM, ticketing system, survey platform, sentiment surveillance tool, account scoring engine, or automated client-decision system.

## Brand Identity

Client Feedback Analyzer belongs to the Ansiversa ecosystem. Every marketing asset should feel simple, trustworthy, intelligent, professional, accessible, and globally understandable.

The brand identity should communicate:

- Simplicity: scattered feedback should become easier to review.
- Trust: source comments and client context should remain visible.
- Intelligence: insights should support decisions without replacing judgment.
- Professionalism: useful for consultants, agencies, freelancers, product teams, and small businesses.
- Accessibility: teams should understand feedback without an enterprise CX stack.
- Evidence-backed action: the emotional center is clarity from client signals.

Avoid:

- Enterprise CX platform claims.
- Sentiment certainty or account health scoring as truth.
- CRM, ticketing, survey, or surveillance positioning.
- AI theme extraction claims as current features.
- Exposing sensitive client/customer comments in marketing.

## Core Value Proposition

Client feedback often lives across calls, emails, notes, chats, and project records. Client Feedback Analyzer gives users a structured workflow for client profiles, feedback, insights, and reports so recommendations stay connected to source evidence.

Its strongest value is reviewable action. Sentiment, priority, and recommendations remain human-review signals rather than unquestioned truth.

## Problems We Solve

- Client feedback is scattered across channels.
- Source comments get separated from insights.
- Teams need to identify recurring themes and follow-up actions.
- Manual sentiment and priority labels need context.
- Small teams do not need enterprise CX complexity.
- Client feedback may contain sensitive business information.
- AI summaries can flatten nuance if source evidence disappears.
