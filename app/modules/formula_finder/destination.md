# Formula Finder Destination

## App Name

Formula Finder

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Formula Finder should become Ansiversa's trusted reference and recall companion
for formulas: a fast, calm place where learners and knowledge workers can find
the right formula, understand when to use it, copy it safely, and connect it to
practice, study, and problem-solving workflows.

At maturity, Formula Finder should not feel like a static formula list. It
should feel like a compact knowledge layer that helps users move from "I know
there is a formula for this" to "I understand what this formula means, how to
use it, and what to practice next."

The product should remain quick enough for a thirty-second lookup, but useful
enough for weekly study, exam preparation, technical refreshers, and cross-app
learning journeys.

## Target Users

- Students studying mathematics, physics, chemistry, finance, and technical
  subjects.
- Teachers and tutors who need a clean reference companion during explanations.
- Self-learners refreshing formulas while studying independently.
- Parents helping children with homework and revision.
- Exam candidates who need fast recall and category-based review.
- Knowledge workers who occasionally need technical, financial, or statistical
  formulas without opening heavyweight tools.

## Core User Problems

- Users forget formula details even when they understand the topic.
- Search-engine results are noisy, inconsistent, and often overcomplicated for
  quick recall.
- Formula lists rarely explain when a formula is useful, what each variable
  means, or what common mistakes to avoid.
- Learners need a bridge from reference lookup to practice, review, and
  long-term retention.
- Users want quick copy/favorite behavior without creating an account for a
  simple lookup.
- Teachers and parents need reliable formula explanations that do not distract
  from the learning moment.

## Final Capabilities

- Search formulas by name, equation, keyword, category, and use case.
- Browse formula categories across mathematics, science, finance, statistics,
  engineering basics, and school-level reference packs.
- View each formula with equation, description, variables, units, assumptions,
  explanation, common mistakes, and simple examples.
- Copy formulas in plain text, display-friendly text, and later structured
  formats where useful.
- Save favorites locally by default, with optional account-backed sync in a
  later governed version.
- Filter by favorites, category, topic, difficulty, subject, and recently used
  formulas.
- Provide worked examples for common formulas without turning the app into a
  full calculator.
- Connect formulas to practice questions, concept explanations, study plans,
  and memory drills.
- Offer offline-friendly bundled reference packs for high-value formula sets.
- Keep formula data reviewed, versioned, and trustworthy.
- Preserve a fast browser-first lookup path even if advanced account-backed
  features are added later.

## Advanced Capabilities

- User-created private formula collections.
- Teacher or tutor-curated formula packs.
- Formula comparison views for closely related formulas.
- Unit-awareness and variable-definition helpers.
- Optional account-backed favorites and recent lookup history.
- Lightweight example builders for selected formulas.
- Printable or exportable formula sheets for study sessions.
- Versioned formula library updates with review notes.
- Domain-specific packs for exams, professional certifications, and workplace
  refreshers.

## AI Opportunities

- Explain a formula in simpler language based on the user's level.
- Suggest related formulas and prerequisite concepts.
- Generate short worked examples from a selected formula.
- Identify likely formulas from a natural-language problem statement.
- Warn users when a formula may not fit the described situation.
- Convert missed quiz questions into recommended formula review.
- Summarize a user's weak formula areas after repeated study or quiz activity.
- Help draft teacher-reviewed formula packs, examples, and common-mistake
  notes.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Quiz: connect missed formula-based questions to relevant formula reference
  cards and targeted practice.
- Concept Explainer: open explanations for the underlying concept behind a
  formula.
- Study Planner: turn weak formula categories into scheduled study tasks.
- Course Tracker: attach formula packs to course modules and review milestones.
- Memory Trainer: convert formulas, variables, and common mistakes into recall
  drills.
- AI Notes Summarizer: extract formula references from study notes and link
  them to the formula library.
- Daily Word Challenge: reuse streak and recall patterns for formula-memory
  practice where appropriate.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return weekly because Formula Finder supports repeated study and recall:
they revisit favorites, review formulas tied to current coursework, refresh
before quizzes or exams, and move from lookup into practice across the
Ansiversa learning ecosystem.

The mature product should make formula recall less stressful by giving users a
reliable place to find, understand, save, and revisit formulas without noise.

## Success Criteria

- Users can find a relevant formula in seconds.
- Formula cards are clear enough to support real understanding, not just copy
  actions.
- Favorites and filters make repeated use faster over time.
- The formula library is trustworthy, reviewed, and not bloated with low-value
  entries.
- Users can move naturally from formula lookup to explanation, practice,
  planning, and memory reinforcement.
- The browser-first experience remains fast and private.
- Advanced features do not make quick lookup slower or more complicated.
- Teachers, students, and self-learners all recognize the product as more than
  a static list.

## Non Goals

Formula Finder is not intended to become:

- A full symbolic algebra engine.
- A heavyweight calculator suite.
- A general search engine for all academic content.
- A social formula-sharing network.
- A public marketplace for unreviewed formula packs.
- A replacement for teachers, tutors, textbooks, or formal proof systems.

These directions should remain out of scope unless the destination itself is
reviewed and intentionally changed.

## Guiding Principles

Every Formula Finder feature should:

- Make formula lookup faster or clearer.
- Improve understanding, not just copying.
- Preserve trust in formula accuracy and context.
- Keep the browser-first path private and lightweight.
- Reduce study friction.
- Fit naturally into the Ansiversa learning ecosystem.
- Avoid turning a focused reference product into a noisy general-purpose tool.

## Governance Notes

This destination is aspirational. It describes the target product direction,
not the current implementation and not an authorization to build every feature
now.

destination.md is not a promise of what will be built next. It is a
description of what the product could ultimately become if time, user value,
and platform direction remain aligned.

Product owner and Astra review are required before accepting, prioritizing, or
implementing any destination item. Particular care is needed before approving
AI formula recommendations, account-backed sync, user-created formula
collections, or worked-example generation because these features affect trust,
privacy, and educational correctness.

Future review gates:

- Favorite sync: later and optional.
- Worked examples: allowed, but must not become calculator behavior.
- AI recommendations: only with trust and review controls.
- Private collections: later, governed carefully.
