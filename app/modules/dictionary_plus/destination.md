# Dictionary+ Destination

## App Name

Dictionary+

## Destination Status

Approved v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Dictionary+ should mature into a personal vocabulary workspace where users can
look up words, save useful vocabulary, organize word lists, review meanings,
and connect vocabulary learning to the wider Ansiversa learning ecosystem.

The product should support word understanding and personal vocabulary growth
without becoming a licensed dictionary publisher, translation platform,
language course, full-text search engine, or automated assessment product.

At its destination, Dictionary+ should feel like a focused reference and
vocabulary notebook: fast for lookup, useful for saving, and structured enough
to support future practice.

## Target Users

- Students learning unfamiliar words while studying.
- Writers checking meaning, usage, and examples.
- Language learners building personal vocabulary lists.
- Readers who want to save words for later review.
- Professionals learning domain-specific vocabulary.
- Non-native English speakers who need learner-friendly explanations and usage nuance.
- Teachers or test-prep users who need vocabulary records they can review without copying publisher content.

## Core User Problems

- Quick word lookups are often forgotten immediately after use.
- Users need to save meaningful words into reusable lists.
- Definitions, examples, pronunciation, and context can become scattered.
- Vocabulary practice needs a personal word library before it can be useful.
- External dictionary features can create licensing, quality, and scope issues.
- Users need to know whether a definition, example, origin, or explanation came from a trusted source, their own note, or AI assistance.
- Similar words, tone, and formality can be hard to compare from one-off search results.

## Final Capabilities

- Record owner-scoped word lookups.
- Store definition, pronunciation, part of speech, and example sentence.
- Save useful words from lookups or manual entry.
- Delete saved words when no longer needed.
- Create and update word lists.
- Toggle saved words into and out of word lists.
- Preserve ordered saved-word membership for personal vocabulary collections.
- Support import and export of personal vocabulary data.
- Provide practice handoffs to Daily Word Challenge or Memory Trainer.
- Keep user vocabulary private and scoped to the owner.

## Advanced Capabilities

- Governed dictionary-provider lookup.
- Pronunciation audio from licensed or approved providers.
- Example usage suggestions with user review.
- Vocabulary review scheduling.
- Import/export for CSV or markdown word lists.
- Learner-friendly view modes for simpler explanations, examples, and usage notes.
- Compare-and-choose support for near-synonyms, tone, formality, and context.
- Recent lookup history with clear privacy controls.
- Daily Word Challenge integration for saved words.
- Memory Trainer handoff for recall practice.
- Lightweight vocabulary analytics such as saved words by part of speech or
  list.

## AI Opportunities

AI can help explain vocabulary and generate examples, but it must respect
dictionary accuracy, licensing, and review boundaries.

Potential AI support includes:

- Rephrasing user-provided definitions in simpler language.
- Suggesting example sentences for saved words.
- Explaining differences between similar words.
- Grouping saved words by theme or difficulty.
- Suggesting review prompts from user-approved saved words.
- Identifying incomplete vocabulary records.

AI must not pretend to be a licensed dictionary source, invent authoritative
etymology, replace verified dictionary providers, or generate language claims
without user review.

## Ecosystem Connections

- Daily Word Challenge can use approved saved words for practice.
- Memory Trainer can receive vocabulary lists for recall games.
- Study Planner can create review tasks for selected word lists.
- Grammar and Paraphrasing Assistant can benefit from saved vocabulary context
  only with explicit user action.
- AI Translator and Tone Fixer may hand off unfamiliar words for vocabulary
  review.
- Course Tracker can link vocabulary lists to language or professional courses.

Dictionary+ owns word records, saved words, and word lists. It should not
absorb translation, grammar rewriting, full language courses, or memory-game
delivery.

## Weekly Return Value

Users return when they encounter new words, save vocabulary from reading or
study, organize lists, review meanings, and send selected words into practice
workflows.

The weekly value is personal vocabulary growth: words become reusable learning
assets instead of one-time lookups.

## Success Criteria

- Users can look up, save, organize, and delete vocabulary records quickly.
- Saved words and word lists remain owner-scoped and easy to manage.
- The app distinguishes user-entered data from provider or AI-assisted data.
- Lookup moments can become reviewable learning assets instead of one-time searches.
- Vocabulary handoffs support practice without turning Dictionary+ into a quiz
  or language course.
- Future provider integrations respect licensing and attribution.
- AI assistance improves clarity without claiming dictionary authority.

## Non Goals

- Do not become a licensed dictionary publisher by default.
- Do not become a translation platform.
- Do not become a full language course.
- Do not become a grammar rewriting tool.
- Do not become a full-text search engine.
- Do not replace Daily Word Challenge or Memory Trainer.
- Do not present AI-generated definitions as authoritative dictionary entries.
- Do not use external dictionary data without governance and licensing review.
- Do not make vocabulary assessment claims without evidence.
- Do not expose lookup history or saved vocabulary unexpectedly.
- Do not interrupt quick lookup with ad-like, paywall-like, or unrelated flows.

## Guiding Principles

- Keep vocabulary personal, reviewable, and owner-scoped.
- Distinguish lookup records from saved learning assets.
- Respect dictionary licensing and source authority.
- Make AI explanations optional and clearly non-authoritative.
- Connect to practice apps through explicit handoffs.
- Favor focused word understanding over broad language-platform scope.
- Keep source authority, licensing, and user ownership visible in language workflows.
- Keep the workflow fast enough for repeated reading and study use.
