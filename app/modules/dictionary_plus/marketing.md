## App Summary

Dictionary+ is an Ansiversa vocabulary app that helps authenticated users record word lookups, save useful words, and organize saved words into personal lists. The current live V1 stores user-entered dictionary data and owner-scoped word collections, not licensed dictionary-provider content.

The product is a personal vocabulary workspace. It is not a licensed dictionary publisher, translation platform, full language course, grammar rewriting tool, or authoritative AI dictionary.

## Brand Identity

Dictionary+ belongs to the Ansiversa ecosystem. Every marketing asset should feel simple, trustworthy, intelligent, professional, accessible, and globally understandable.

The brand identity should communicate:

- Simplicity: words should be easy to record, save, and organize.
- Trust: user vocabulary should be private and owner-scoped.
- Intelligence: lookup moments should become reusable learning assets.
- Professionalism: useful for students, writers, readers, language learners, and professionals.
- Accessibility: vocabulary work should feel clear and learner-friendly.
- Personal growth: the emotional center is remembering useful words.

Avoid:

- Authoritative dictionary coverage claims without licensed content.
- AI-generated definitions as dictionary authority.
- Translation, grammar rewriting, or full language course positioning.
- Copying dictionary definitions, examples, pronunciation data, or word lists.
- Exposing private lookup history.

## Core Value Proposition

Quick lookups are useful for the moment, but easy to forget. Dictionary+ turns word encounters into owner-scoped records with definitions, pronunciation, part of speech, examples, saved words, and word lists.

Its strongest value is personal vocabulary memory. V1 supports user-entered vocabulary records and lists while keeping future provider lookup, pronunciation audio, AI examples, and practice handoffs governed.

## Problems We Solve

- Users look up words once and forget them.
- Saved vocabulary gets scattered across notes and apps.
- Definitions, pronunciation, examples, and context need a shared record.
- Users need word lists before practice workflows become useful.
- Licensed dictionary content requires careful source governance.
- AI explanations can invent definitions or word origins if not reviewed.
