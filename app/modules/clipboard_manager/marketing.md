## App Summary

Clipboard Manager is an Ansiversa utility app that gives authenticated users a private browser-local workspace for saving useful text snippets, searching saved history, and copying entries back to the clipboard.

The current V1 product is approved live at version `1.0.0`. It is intentionally local-first: clipboard entries are stored in the user's browser localStorage and are not uploaded to the backend, synced across devices, monitored in the background, or read automatically.

Clipboard Manager helps users keep reusable text close at hand without turning the clipboard into a cloud service.

## Core Value Proposition

People copy and reuse small pieces of text every day: replies, notes, commands, links, templates, IDs, and reminders. Those snippets often get lost in temporary clipboard history, scattered notes, or chat drafts.

Clipboard Manager gives users a focused workspace for saving reusable text intentionally. It supports manual entry, explicit paste, search, copy, delete, clear-all controls, and lightweight local insights without sending clipboard content to the backend.

## Problems We Solve

- Useful copied text disappears after the next copy action.
- Repeated replies and small templates are scattered across notes and messages.
- Users need a lightweight place for reusable snippets.
- Clipboard tools can feel risky when they monitor or sync sensitive data.
- Some users want local control instead of cloud clipboard storage.
- Searchable snippet history should not require a heavy notes app.
- Future sync and import/export need clear privacy governance before promotion.

## Marketing Guardrails

- Do not claim cloud sync, encrypted sync, device sync, import/export, categories, pins, expiration rules, or AI rewriting as current features.
- Do not present Clipboard Manager as a password manager, secrets vault, compliance archive, or enterprise clipboard monitoring tool.
- Do not imply the app reads clipboard content automatically.
- Do not imply background clipboard monitoring.
- Do not imply browser extension functionality.
- Do not show passwords, credentials, private keys, medical data, legal records, financial records, or personal secrets in marketing visuals.
- Do not claim clipboard content is stored on the backend.
- Keep the central promise simple: save selected text snippets locally, search them, and copy them when needed.
