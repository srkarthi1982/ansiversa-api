## Purpose

Understand the personal vehicle document organization market enough to build a focused Ansiversa V1 without copying competitor workflows or making official-system claims.

## Problem Statement

Drivers often need to remember registration, insurance, inspection, warranty, and service-contract dates. Existing solutions can be scattered across calendar reminders, notes, spreadsheets, insurance portals, government services, and fleet tools.

## Target Users

- Individual vehicle owners.
- Families managing multiple vehicles.
- Users who prefer manual reminders over automated portals.
- People who want metadata organization without uploading sensitive files.

## Competitor Landscape

The market includes car maintenance apps, insurance apps, government renewal portals, document vaults, calendar reminder tools, spreadsheet templates, and fleet-management systems. Many tools either focus on official renewal, broader vehicle maintenance, or document storage rather than simple metadata tracking.

## Common Market Features

- Vehicle profiles.
- Registration and insurance expiry reminders.
- Inspection or emissions certificate tracking.
- Document number/reference fields.
- Calendar reminders and status labels.
- Optional document upload in more mature products.

## User Love Signals

Users value simplicity, clear reminders, multi-vehicle support, and a fast view of what expires next.

## Complaints and Friction

Users may distrust uploading sensitive documents, dislike overly complex fleet features, or get confused when an app sounds like an official renewal portal.

## Pricing and Paywall Observations

Document storage, cloud sync, family sharing, and notification automation are often monetized in adjacent tools. Simple metadata tracking is commonly expected to be lightweight.

## AI Trends

AI/OCR can extract data from uploaded documents, but it raises privacy and accuracy concerns. V1 avoids OCR and document scanning.

## UX Patterns

Effective products use date-focused lists, upcoming-expiry badges, vehicle grouping, and simple filters by type/status/vehicle.

## Opportunities

Ansiversa can provide a calm personal tracker that stays clear about its boundary: organize your own records, watch dates, and avoid official compliance or renewal promises.

## Avoid List

- Do not copy government or insurance portal language.
- Do not imply automatic renewal or legal verification.
- Do not require uploads in V1.
- Do not overbuild fleet-management workflows.

## Product Questions

- Should future versions integrate with a secure document vault?
- Should reminder notifications be in-app only or external?
- Should households share vehicle records?

## Sources

General public vehicle document, maintenance, calendar reminder, document vault, government renewal, and insurance portal patterns reviewed at a category level. No competitor wording, templates, screenshots, or proprietary flows were copied.

## Review Notes

Initial V1 scope should remain metadata-only and manual-entry first.
