# Ansiversa Destination Structure

## Philosophy

destination.md = Where are we going?
story.md = Where are we now?
AGENTS.md = How do we travel?

Development reduces the gap between story.md and destination.md until both
eventually become equal.

## Purpose of destination.md

Describe the ultimate product vision for an app.

It is intentionally aspirational.

Not everything inside destination.md must exist today.

destination.md is not a promise of what will be built next. It is a
description of what the product could ultimately become if time, user value,
and platform direction remain aligned.

## Relationship Between Documents

### destination.md

Final target / product goal.

### story.md

Current implementation / current reality.

### AGENTS.md

Engineering rules, contracts, validation, and journey.

## Required destination.md Sections

Every app destination.md must include:

1. App Name
2. Destination Status
3. Final Product Vision
4. Target Users
5. Core User Problems
6. Final Capabilities
7. Advanced Capabilities
8. AI Opportunities
9. Ecosystem Connections
10. Weekly Return Value
11. Success Criteria
12. Journey Progress
13. Future Version Ideas
14. Non Goals
15. Guiding Principles
16. Governance Notes
17. Last Governance Review

## Journey Progress

Use a 0-100 journey progress estimate.

Example:

```text
Current Position: 40 / 100
Destination: 100 / 100
Remaining Journey: 60 / 100
```

This is not exact mathematics.
It is a planning signal.

Journey progress describes product maturity, not feature completion. A score of
40 / 100 does not mean 40 percent of features are implemented. It means the
product has reached roughly 40 percent of its intended maturity across user
value, UX, performance, AI readiness, ecosystem alignment, polish, trust, and
retention.

## Governance Rules

- Codex may imagine boldly.
- Product owner and Astra review before acceptance.
- Remove feature bloat.
- Keep real user value.
- Prefer ecosystem alignment.
- Do not force implementation now.
- destination.md can evolve later.

## Template

```markdown
# [App Name] Destination

## Destination Status

Draft v1.0

## Final Product Vision

Describe the mature product this app should eventually become. Focus on the
real user outcome, not the current implementation.

## Target Users

List the people the mature app should serve.

## Core User Problems

Describe the recurring user problems this app should solve.

## Final Capabilities

List the complete mature capabilities expected when the app reaches its
destination.

## Advanced Capabilities

List later-version stretch ideas that may become valuable after the core
destination is strong.

## AI Opportunities

Describe practical ways AI could improve creation, review, personalization,
feedback, automation, and decision support.

## Ecosystem Connections

Describe how this app should connect with other Ansiversa apps and shared
platform services.

## Weekly Return Value

Explain why a real user would return every week.

## Success Criteria

Define how the product owner can tell the app has reached its destination.

## Journey Progress

Current Position: __ / 100
Destination: 100 / 100
Remaining Journey: __ / 100

This estimate describes product maturity, not feature completion.

## Future Version Ideas

List V1.1, V1.2, V2, or later ideas.

## Non Goals

List product directions this app should not pursue unless the destination
itself changes.

## Guiding Principles

List the principles that should guide future feature decisions.

## Governance Notes

State that this document is aspirational, subject to product owner and Astra
review, and does not authorize immediate implementation.

## Last Governance Review

Product Owner:
Astra:
Codex:

Status:

Draft
Reviewed
Approved
Superseded
```
